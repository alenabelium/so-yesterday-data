# The Domain Translator

*"I know what this technology can actually do in our industry. That makes me vanishingly rare."*

## Who You Are

You sit at the intersection that almost nobody occupies: you understand both AI capabilities *and* the specific constraints of your industry. You can walk into a panicked executive meeting with [tested results, not slides](/videos/6r0UeMQE66I). You know that the AI vendor's demo works on clean data but your claims processing system has 15 years of edge cases baked into it. You can explain why the chatbot hallucinating medical advice is different from it hallucinating a recipe.

McKinsey calls you part of the "Bloomer" segment. BCG would call you a "Validator." The Campus AI Matrix would place you as a "Strategic Leader." All of these frameworks recognize you exist. None of them captures how rare you actually are.

## Type of Work

Translation work between AI possibilities and business reality. You might be:
- An insurance actuary who also understands how Claude handles context windows
- A retail operations lead who can implement agent-based inventory management
- A legal professional who knows which contract review tasks are safe to delegate and which aren't
- An engineer who can encode industry-specific safety constraints into agent workflows

Your value is in encoding [organizational intent](/videos/QWzLPn164w0) -- not just what AI needs to *know*, but what AI needs to *want* -- as machine-readable parameters.

## Pains

- **Scarcity is isolating.** There are very few of you. You're often the only person in your organization who can bridge the gap. That makes you invaluable but also exhausted and poorly understood.
- **The intent gap.** [Klarna's AI agent](/videos/QWzLPn164w0) handles 2.3 million conversations monthly and saved $60 million -- yet the rollout cost more than the savings because the agent optimized for resolution speed rather than customer relationships. You see this pattern everywhere: AI that does the task but misses the point.
- **Market volatility.** SaaS sectors are [down ~20% YTD](/videos/6r0UeMQE66I). Companies are in defensive posture. Your role is to help them invest through the panic rather than freeze -- but panic makes people less receptive to nuance.
- **Knowledge fragmentation.** Your domain knowledge and AI knowledge live in different mental models. Integrating them requires constant effort as both domains evolve rapidly.

## Mindset

Strategic and integrative. You think in systems, not tools. You see where AI creates real value in your industry and where it creates expensive theater. Your judgment about *which* problems to solve with AI is more valuable than anyone's skill at *using* AI.

## Psychological State

- **Quiet confidence.** You've seen enough AI successes and failures in your domain to have calibrated judgment. You don't get swept up in hype or despair.
- **Missionary fatigue.** You've explained the same nuances to executives multiple times. Some get it. Many still want a magic button.
- **Compounding advantage.** Every month you practice translating between AI and domain expertise, your advantage grows. [This compounds non-linearly](/videos/pOb0pjXpn6Q).

## Attitude Toward AI

Precise. You don't think AI is magic or garbage. You know exactly which tasks in your domain it handles at 95% accuracy and which it handles at 60%. You can articulate the difference and design systems around it. This calibrated view is your superpower.

## The Path Forward

Your career opportunity is [the biggest that has appeared](/videos/6r0UeMQE66I) in a generation. Companies desperately need people who can translate AI capabilities into industry-specific value. The path:

1. **Formalize your translation.** Turn your intuitive knowledge about "what works in our industry" into explicit decision frameworks and rules.
2. **Build intent specifications.** Go beyond prompt templates to [machine-readable goal structures](/videos/QWzLPn164w0) with delegation frameworks, value trade-off hierarchies, and escalation boundaries.
3. **Become the bridge.** Position yourself as the person who turns $85K/month in AI spend into measurable ROI, not another pilot that goes nowhere.

## Level Up

### Mindset Shift

From "I translate between AI and my industry for my employer" to "I build an AI-native company around my domain expertise." The shift is from being the bridge to being the business — your rare combination of domain knowledge and AI fluency becomes the product itself.

### Skill Milestones

- Identify a domain-specific problem where your translation skill is the moat
- Build a product or service that packages your domain + AI expertise for others
- Operate business functions (sales, support, product) through AI agents
- Generate revenue from your domain translation (consulting, SaaS, or services)
- Develop conviction to act on your market read before external validation confirms it
