# Daily Digest — March 25, 2026

The 5 most important AI developments from recent content.

## 1. McKinsey Predicts $1 Trillion in AI Agent Commerce, Forcing Data Stack Overhaul

McKinsey forecasts that $1 trillion in sales will shift to autonomous AI agents, rendering businesses with unstructured data invisible to the new commerce layer. Companies must urgently refactor internal systems to be 'agent-readable and writable,' converting tribal knowledge into durable schemas to capture this revenue.

A major update to Claude Code Skills allows agents to automatically build, test, and evaluate custom skills using blind A/B comparisons against baseline performance. This breakthrough enables developers to create highly specialized workflows that self-optimize while utilizing dynamic context injection to reduce token waste.

## 3. Strategic Shift: Internal Engineering Teams Replace AI Consulting Firms

New analysis suggests that enterprise-grade AI agent systems can be built internally by applying timeless software engineering principles like data dominance and strict linting, rather than relying on expensive consultants. This shift validates that clean infrastructure and simple architectures are more effective for agentic systems than complex new paradigms.

Sources: [Your Engineers Can Build What Accenture Charges You Millions For.](/videos/7AO4w4Y_L24)

## 4. Global AI Infrastructure Race Intensifies Over Energy and Semiconductor Bottlenecks

Eric Schmidt warns that while the US leads in software, China is dominating physical robotics due to vertical integration, creating a critical gap in hardware capabilities. Simultaneously, severe bottlenecks in 2nm semiconductor capacity and energy deficits are driving a race for fusion power and orbital data centers to sustain recursive AI growth.

Sources: [Eric Schmidt: America’s Energy Problem, China’s Winning the Physical AI Race, & AGI Countdown](/videos/DpwmmXmzvfo), [Welcome to March 23, 2026](/videos/OL6FgRs3AWo)

## 5. New Framework for Evaluating AI Agents: Execution, Orchestration, and Interface

A new strategic framework has emerged to decode the trade-offs in the 'OpenClaw' era of AI agents, focusing on execution location (local vs. cloud), intelligence orchestration, and interface contracts. This allows organizations to select agents based on specific needs for data sovereignty versus delegated trust rather than feature parity.
