## 2. The Rise of Automated 'Karpathy Loops' for Self-Optimizing AI Systems

A new pattern called the 'Karpathy loop' is enabling AI agents to autonomously iterate on their own code and system prompts against specific metrics, achieving significant performance gains overnight. This 'local hard takeoff' creates an asymmetric advantage for small, agile teams that can deploy these loops, while large enterprises struggle to adapt due to a lack of necessary evaluation infrastructure.

A critical global shortage of DRAM is driving up manufacturing costs and forcing major chipmakers like Google and Intel to innovate with new memory processing units to sustain the AI buildout. This scarcity is treating frontier AI models as critical infrastructure, prompting government agencies to actively manage supply chains and red-team systems amidst the resource constraints.
