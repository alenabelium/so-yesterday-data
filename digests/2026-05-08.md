## 2. The Strategic Shift to Semantic Work Primitives

Industry leaders argue that AI agents fail at real work not due to model limitations, but because software lacks 'semantic work primitives' that define the meaning and authority of actions. The future of AI transformation requires moving beyond UI-centric designs to expose rich, permissioned units of work, allowing agents to understand intent and context rather than just manipulating visual interfaces.

## 4. The 'Anticipation Gap' and the Ladder of Permission

Consumer AI faces a critical 'anticipation gap' where agents act as management burdens rather than assistants because they lack the intuition to navigate messy, unstructured personal lives. To bridge this, a new 'ladder of permission' framework is emerging, requiring agents to prove reliability in specific domains before progressing from suggesting actions to acting autonomously without constant human supervision.
