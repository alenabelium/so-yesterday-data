# Daily Digest — May 17, 2026

The 5 most important AI developments from recent content.

## 1. Shift to Agentic Workflows and Parallel Agent Management

The industry is rapidly moving from single-model interactions to autonomous multi-agent systems, evidenced by Meta's Muse Spark using 16 parallel agents and Anthropic's introduction of 'Agent Views' for managing concurrent sessions. This shift prioritizes workflow execution and implementation layers over raw model intelligence, as generic models become commoditized and value concentrates in custom integration and governance.

## 2. New SaaS Pricing Models for Autonomous Agents

Major vendors like Salesforce, Microsoft, and ServiceNow are introducing 'agentic work units' as a second billing layer alongside traditional per-seat licenses, charging for operational tasks rather than just access. This transition forces procurement teams to negotiate complex contracts regarding caps, exportable logs, and failed action costs to avoid financial risks as agents become mission-critical.

## 3. Breakthroughs in Multimodal Drug Discovery with 'Mammal'

The launch of Mammal, a unified foundation model integrating chemistry, genetics, and protein data, has demonstrated superior performance in predicting drug efficacy and antibody binding compared to specialized models like AlphaFold 3. Its ability to correctly predict new uses for existing drugs, confirmed by physical experiments, signals a major acceleration in personalized medicine and disease treatment capabilities.
