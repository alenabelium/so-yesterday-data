## 4. Strategic Shift to Local-First AI Infrastructure

The rise of autonomous agents is reversing the cloud-only trend, necessitating local hardware with high unified memory to manage personal data, persistent memory layers, and privacy-sensitive tasks. Enterprises and individuals are pivoting to durable, owned stacks where local agents handle core workflows, using cloud models only for specialized, non-sensitive compute bursts.

Companies must audit and clean their existing CRMs and ERPs to ensure they possess the 'substrate' of work records that autonomous agents need to coordinate complex tasks.
