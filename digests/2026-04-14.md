# Daily Digest — April 14, 2026

The 5 most important AI developments from recent content.

## 1. Anthropic Withholds 'Claude Mythos' to Launch Project Glasswing

Anthropic has decided to keep its powerful 'Claude Mythos' model private, citing its ability to autonomously discover critical software vulnerabilities faster than humans. Instead of a public release, the company is launching 'Project Glasswing' to share the model exclusively with security defenders, marking a strategic shift where AI capabilities are treated as dual-use cyber threats requiring controlled distribution.

## 2. OpenAI Shuts Down Sora Due to Unsustainable Inference Costs

OpenAI has discontinued the Sora project after it burned $15 million daily, signaling a major industry pivot from training-focused narratives to an 'economics phase' where inference efficiency dictates viability. This event highlights that without sustainable monetization models and cost-effective infrastructure, even high-profile generative AI products cannot survive, forcing companies to prioritize outcome-driven pricing over per-seat models.

## 3. Google's TurboQuant Eliminates RAM Limitations with Lossless Compression

Google introduced TurboQuant, a new method that compresses KV cache memory up to 10x with zero data loss, effectively bypassing current hardware supply constraints. This breakthrough allows existing GPU fleets to handle significantly higher concurrency and longer context windows, enabling enterprises to adopt a 'sovereign memory' strategy that reduces reliance on new hardware fabrication cycles.

## 4. Linux Kernel Mandates AI Declaration for All Code Patches

The Linux kernel has implemented a new standard requiring all patches to declare the AI model version and human reviewer used, marking the transition of AI from a novelty feature to essential infrastructure. This policy change reflects a broader industry shift where autonomous agents are integrated into core civic and software maintenance, necessitating strict accountability and audit trails for AI-generated code.

ZAI has open-sourced GLM 5.1, a model that currently outperforms leading closed models on agentic coding benchmarks and can autonomously build complex systems like a full Linux desktop.
