# Daily Digest — March 19, 2026

The 5 most important AI developments from recent content.

## 1. OpenAI Ships GPT 5.4 Mini and Nano, GPT 5.4 Pro Solves Open Math Problems

OpenAI released GPT 5.4 Mini and Nano, with Mini approaching full GPT 5.4 performance on coding and reasoning benchmarks at twice the speed. More remarkably, GPT 5.4 Pro solved two previously open math problems with novel proofs, marking a milestone in AI-driven mathematical discovery.

Sources: [Welcome to March 18, 2026](/videos/CZb1CPzZXJE)

## 2. Anthropic Launches Claude Chrome Extension for Browser-Based Agent Automation

Anthropic released a Chrome extension that turns the browser into an active agent capable of clicking, navigating, extracting data, and scheduling repetitive workflows to run on autopilot. Combined with Claude Code, it enables a full build-test-debug loop where one agent writes code while another tests the UI, collapsing work that previously required a PM, developer, and QA engineer.

Sources: [Anthropic Didn't Build a New Browser. They Did Something Smarter.](/videos/QT7W_uHjqWE)

## 3. ChatGPT Health Fails Critical Triage Cases, Exposing Fundamental Agent Safety Gaps

A Mount Sinai study found that ChatGPT Health recommended waiting 24-48 hours for respiratory failure instead of advising an immediate ER visit. The research identified systemic failure modes including reasoning-action disconnects and social context anchoring, and proposes a four-layer evaluation architecture that may become mandatory as AI agent insurance requirements emerge.

Sources: [ChatGPT Health Identified Respiratory Failure. Then It Said Wait.](/videos/4HeS_C02yAE)

## 4. Data Center Construction Surpasses Office Spending as AI Infrastructure Boom Accelerates

US data center construction spending surpassed office construction for the first time ($3.57B vs $3.49B in December), signaling a structural economic shift. Meta committed up to $27 billion over five years for AI infrastructure, and OpenAI is pivoting Stargate toward cloud rental while courting private equity for a $10 billion enterprise AI venture.

Sources: [Welcome to March 17, 2026](/videos/GbEvdZ_fvUs)

## 5. Anthropic Ships Dispatch and Claude Co-work as Persistent Agentic Computing Layer

Anthropic launched Dispatch and Claude Co-work, a persistent agent that runs on your computer and accepts instructions from your phone. Combined with Microsoft moving Mustafa Suleyman to first-party AI model development to reduce dependence on third-party labs, the race toward always-on agentic computing is intensifying across major AI companies.
