## 2. Emergence of the 'Bring Your Own Context' (BYOC) Strategy for Portable AI Identity

As AI agents accumulate deep knowledge of individual workflows, workers face the risk of losing this 'working intelligence' when switching jobs due to proprietary platform lock-in. The new BYOC strategy advocates for extracting personal context into portable databases accessible via the Model Context Protocol (MCP), treating AI context as a critical, transferable form of professional capital.

## 3. Infrastructure Bottleneck: The Urgent Need for 'Agent-Native' Software Primitives

Despite exponential gains in model speed, human-centric web infrastructure and APIs are creating a severe bottleneck that prevents AI agents from achieving superhuman efficiency. Experts argue that the software stack must be rebuilt with agent-native primitives, such as persistent containers and shared KV caches, to eliminate human affordances like visual processing and manual authentication.

## 4. Post-Labor Economic Framework: Shifting Income from Wages to Capital Ownership

With AI and robotics unbundling jobs into tasks that machines perform better, cheaper, and faster, the traditional wage-based economy is facing a 'deflationary death spiral.' The proposed solution involves a structural shift where household income is sustained through government transfers, sovereign wealth funds, and employee stock ownership plans rather than traditional employment.

This development lowers the barrier to entry for AI adoption, allowing developers to build full-stack applications like Jira clones without incurring cloud API costs, provided they have adequate local GPU resources.
