# Daily Digest — March 16, 2026

The 5 most important AI developments from recent content.

## 1. The first open-source agentic AI physicist is released

Physical Super Intelligence (PSI), co-founded by Alex Wissner-Gross, released "Get Physics Done" (GPD) — the first open-source AI agent that can scope a physics problem, plan the research, execute derivations, run numerical verification, and package results autonomously. Supporting 16+ subfields from quantum field theory to astrophysics under Apache 2.0, GPD compresses the research cycle from weeks to hours and frames physics as the key domino for downstream breakthroughs in chemistry, energy, and materials science.

Sources: [The First Open-Source Agentic AI Physicist](/videos/ey9m8v7YDYQ)

## 2. Solo founding hits one-third of all new US ventures as AI removes the overhead barrier

Carta data shows solo-founded startups now account for a third of all new US ventures, up from a quarter — driven by AI eliminating the coordination overhead that always blocked extraordinary people. A Harvard Business School experiment at P&G found individuals with AI were 3x more likely to produce top-10% quality ideas. The implication for companies: remove overhead or lose your best talent to solo founding.

Sources: [She quit, picked up AI, and shipped in 30 days what her team planned for Q3.](/videos/zCW-k5fFRgQ)

## 3. Mustafa Suleyman sets a two-year timeline for economically autonomous AI agents

Microsoft's AI CEO predicted agents will pass his "modern Turing test" — autonomously turning $100K into $1M — by 2027. He drew a firm line against AI legal personhood, arguing replicable agents with perfect memory would outcompete biological humans, and advocated for defensive co-scaling of safety alongside capability as the pragmatic middle ground.

Sources: [A Conversation with Mustafa Suleyman](/videos/5iZn9sI3-Hc)

## 4. Four AI labs independently converge on the same multi-agent architecture

Anthropic, Google DeepMind, OpenAI, and Cursor all built the same decompose-parallelize-verify-iterate pattern without coordinating — suggesting this is a discovered solution to scaling intelligence, not a design choice. The convergence implies AI's "jagged frontier" was an artifact of single-turn interaction, and that "sniff-checking" (evaluating whether output is correct) is becoming the most valuable human skill across every job family.

Sources: [4 AI Labs Built the Same System Without Talking to Each Other](/videos/LO0Ws-l6brg)

## 5. Meta reportedly in talks to license Google's Gemini after its own model delays

Meta is reportedly considering licensing Google's Gemini after its in-house "Avocado" model fell behind schedule — a striking signal that even trillion-dollar labs cannot guarantee frontier model delivery on time. The potential deal underscores that the AI race is moving too fast for any single organization to go it alone, and cross-competitor licensing may become standard practice.
