Create an engaging video overview about how AI is rewriting the business model playbook — shifting from the platform economy to the intelligence economy.

Focus on THREE main points:

1. **The SaaS extinction event.** When Anthropic released a 200-line contract review plugin, $285 billion in SaaS market cap evaporated in hours. Per-seat pricing is dying — it dropped from 21% to 15% of SaaS companies in twelve months. Klarna replaced Salesforce entirely with an internal AI system that handled 2.3 million customer conversations in its first month. The model is inverting from "Software-as-a-Service" (here's a tool, do the work yourself) to "Service-as-Software" (here's the outcome, it's done). Sierra charges only when AI actually resolves a customer issue. Intercom charges $0.99 per resolution — not per seat, not per month, per result.

2. **The five-person, $200M company and agent-mediated commerce.** AI-native companies operate at 3-5x revenue per employee versus traditional SaaS. The cost of intelligence is falling 10-200x annually. Amazon is quietly converting headcount into compute capacity. And the really strange part: McKinsey projects $900B-$1T in agent-mediated commerce in US retail by 2030 — your customer might not be human. Your personal shopping agent negotiates with a retailer's AI agent. No browser tabs, no comparison shopping, no human in the loop. Marketing funnels get compressed into API calls.

3. **The four stages of transformation and the new scarcity.** MIT identifies four stages: bolt-on AI (where 90% of companies are stuck), customer proxy, modular creator, and orchestrator. Most companies are at Stage 1 because they think AI is a feature, not a business model. The essay argues that the scarce resource of the intelligence economy is taste — human judgment to know what's worth building and when to override the machine. Intelligence is becoming a commodity. Knowing what to do with it is the moat.

Tone: Business strategy — authoritative but accessible. Think Harvard Business Review meets a TED talk. Use the Uber/Airbnb analogy as a frame: platforms unlocked idle physical assets, AI unlocks idle cognitive assets. Make the listener feel the urgency — this shift is happening now, not in five years.

Target audience: Business leaders, startup founders, product managers, and strategists who need to understand how AI changes their business model — not just their tools.
