# Two reference models

The essay's central directive is: *"Estonia's deployment speed on Finland's pedagogy."* This file gives the video the concrete grounding behind both halves.

## A. Estonia's AI Leap

**Programme name:** AI Leap (Estonian: *TI Hüpe*).

**Launch date:** 1 September 2025.

**Phase 1 cohort:** 20,000 secondary-school students + 3,000 teachers.

**What students and teachers get:** free access to leading frontier AI tools, paired with teacher training.

**Partners:** public-private — Estonian state + private partners as co-founders of a new **AI Leap Foundation**. Tool partnerships with OpenAI and Anthropic.

**Governance:** the AI Leap Foundation is being established as the apolitical implementing body; this matters because it is explicitly designed to outlast electoral cycles.

**Initiator:** Estonian President Alar Karis convened the founding roundtable in December 2024 with key Estonian tech entrepreneurs (including Jaan Tallinn, Kaarel Kotkas, Taavet Hinrikus) and the Ministry of Education.

**Stated philosophy:** *deploy tools first, let curriculum self-organise around what works — but pair access with teacher training from day one.* This is a refinement of the earlier instinct ("Estonia did not wait for a perfect curriculum"); access without teacher support fails on the three-week cliff.

**Why Slovenia should care:**
- Estonia is small (population 1.4M), postsocialist, European.
- It did this within a single budget cycle, with €15M-scale funding rather than billions.
- Its public-private model gives Slovenia a turn-key governance template.
- The vendors that signed up for Estonia (OpenAI, Anthropic) will sign up for Slovenia for the same reasons — small country = visible, ambitious deployment = marketing asset.

**Video angle:** the *speed* of the precedent matters more than the size. Estonia from roundtable (December 2024) to deployment (September 2025) was **nine months**. Slovenia has time to do the same by September 2026 — if it starts now.

Source: <https://e-estonia.com/ai-leap-2025-estonia-sets-ai-standard-in-education/>

## B. Finland's phenomenon-based learning

**What it is:** a pedagogical approach in which schools organise teaching around **real-world phenomena** — climate, migration, energy, media, public health — across the boundaries of traditional subjects.

**Regulatory anchor:** included in the Finnish **core curriculum for basic education, 2014**, in force from 2016.

**Obligation:** every basic school for ages 7–16 must run at least one **multi-disciplinary, phenomenon-based learning period** each school year. It is **not** a replacement for subjects; it sits alongside them.

**Pedagogical basis:** constructivist. Students learn by investigating connections rather than absorbing pre-categorised content. The explicit aim is to strengthen the learner's *agency* — their capacity to act on the world.

**Why it matches agentic AI:**

An agentic AI system, by its nature, draws across history, chemistry, statistics, law, economics, ethics in a single conversation. A pedagogy that organises learning around phenomena rather than subjects is the **native habitat** for this kind of tool. A teacher trained in phenomenon-based units is, without any additional training, well-positioned to integrate AI tools into the classroom — because she is already thinking across subject lines.

This is the deeper, slower half of the Slovenian recommendation: don't just deploy fast; deploy onto a pedagogy that knows what to do with the tool.

**Caveat for the narration:** Finland's reform did *not* replace subjects with phenomena. It added periods. The video must not overclaim ("Finland abolished subjects" is a common journalist's error — avoid).

Source: <https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/2331186X.2024.2309733>

## C. How to use both in the video

The two models are introduced **together**, not sequentially. The line is: *"Estonia showed us how fast. Finland showed us how deep. Slovenia has no time to choose — it must take both."*

Visually, the two models should anchor the geographic logic of the essay: a small Nordic-Baltic neighbour that already did the thing (Estonia) and a small Nordic neighbour that already built the pedagogy for it (Finland). Slovenia is positioned between them — same demographic scale, same EU policy environment, no excuses.

## D. What Slovenia has *already* that the video should name

- **NpUI 2020–2025** (€110M public funding for AI strategy) — credible existing groundwork.
- A small, educated, multilingual population.
- A high-density SME economy where the marginal gain from agentic tools is large.
- EuroHPC presence (Vega supercomputer) — compute capacity exists.

The video should mention these as the *base* Slovenia already stands on, before introducing the six moves. The country is not starting from zero. It is starting from "credible but stalled."
