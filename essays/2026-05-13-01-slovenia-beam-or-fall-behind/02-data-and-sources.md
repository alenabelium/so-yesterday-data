# Verified data for the video

The narration must use these figures exactly, citing the listed source in spoken context where natural. **No improvisation, no rounding away from the listed value, no "industry surveys" filler.**

## 1. EU and Slovenia — AI in enterprises, 2025

- **20.0 %** of EU enterprises with 10+ employees used AI in 2025. *(Eurostat, 11 Dec 2025)*
- Top: **Denmark 42.0 %**, **Finland 37.8 %**, **Sweden 35.0 %**.
- Bottom: **Romania 5.2 %**, **Poland 8.4 %**, **Bulgaria 8.6 %**.
- **Slovenia: ~22 %** (above the EU headline).
- Slovenia by enterprise size:
  - **Small (10–49 employees): 18 %**
  - **Medium (50–249): 31 %**
  - **Large (250+): 72 %**
- **Gap large vs. small: ~54 percentage points — the widest in the EU.**
- Most common use of AI in Slovenian enterprises: ICT security (narrow, defensive — not a productivity use).
- Main EU-wide barrier to AI adoption: lack of relevant expertise (**70.9 %** of enterprises that considered AI).

Sources: <https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/statistics-explained/index.php?title=Use_of_artificial_intelligence_in_enterprises> · <https://www.stat.si/StatWeb/en/News/Index/13906> · <https://www.stat.si/StatWeb/en/News/Index/14010>

## 2. Reskilling pressure (WEF Future of Jobs Report 2025)

- **~60 %** of workers will need reskilling or upskilling by 2030.
- **11 of every 100** are unlikely to receive it — over **120 million workers** at medium-term redundancy risk.
- **22 %** of jobs will be structurally disrupted by 2030 (170M new, 92M lost → net +78M).
- **77 %** of employers plan to reskill workers; **41 %** plan workforce reductions due to AI automation.

Source: <https://www.weforum.org/publications/the-future-of-jobs-report-2025/>

## 3. Automation exposure (OECD Employment Outlook 2023)

- **~28 % of employment** is in occupations at the highest risk of automation.
- Occupations highly exposed to AI now account for roughly **one third of all job vacancies** in the OECD sample.

Source: <https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/oecd-employment-outlook-2023_08785bba-en.html>

## 4. Microsoft WorkLab — adoption thresholds

- **11 minutes of daily time savings** is the threshold where most users start seeing AI as useful.
- **Around 11 weeks** of sustained use is the breakthrough point where Copilot users report broader gains in productivity, work enjoyment, work-life balance and meeting relief.

Source: <https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/worklab/ai-data-drop-the-11-by-11-tipping-point>

## 5. Shopify "Reflexive AI" memo (April 2025)

- Tobi Lütke memo: **"Reflexive AI usage is now a baseline expectation at Shopify."**
- Before requesting additional headcount, teams must **demonstrate why they cannot get the work done using AI**.
- AI-usage questions added to performance and peer-review process.

Source: <https://www.cnbc.com/2025/04/07/shopify-ceo-prove-ai-cant-do-jobs-before-asking-for-more-headcount.html>

## 6. Estonia — AI Leap programme

- **Launch:** 1 September 2025.
- **First cohort: 20,000 secondary-school students + 3,000 teachers.**
- **Partners:** OpenAI and Anthropic (public-private partnership).
- **Governance:** new AI Leap Foundation; state and private sector as co-founders.
- **Plan:** extend further across the secondary system and into broader public-sector use.

Source: <https://e-estonia.com/ai-leap-2025-estonia-sets-ai-standard-in-education/>

## 7. Finland — phenomenon-based learning

- Incorporated into the **Finnish core curriculum for basic education, 2014** (effective 2016).
- Every basic school for ages 7–16 must run at least one **multi-disciplinary, phenomenon-based learning period** each school year.
- Pedagogy: study real-world phenomena (climate, migration, energy, media, public health) across subject boundaries — **alongside**, not in place of, traditional subjects.

Source: <https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/2331186X.2024.2309733>

## 8. Slovenia — baseline policy

- **NpUI 2020–2025**: €110 million in public funding for AI strategy. Coming to the end of its programmatic horizon.

Source: <https://ai-watch.ec.europa.eu/countries/slovenia/slovenia-ai-strategy-report_en>

## 9. EU funding frame — "Making Europe an AI Continent" (2025)

- EU AI Continent strategy, Digital Decade programme, and EuroHPC AI Factories pillar all prioritise: AI skills, SME adoption, public-sector use, compute access.
- **None earmark** "buy every citizen a frontier-AI seat." All three fund adjacent and complementary moves.

Source: <https://www.europarl.europa.eu/RegData/etudes/BRIE/2025/775923/EPRS_BRI(2025)775923_EN.pdf>

## 10. Klarna walkback (2025)

- 2024: AI assistant initially absorbed the workload of **~700 customer-service agents**.
- 2025: Klarna **walked some of that back** after CEO said AI-only support produced **lower-quality output** and that customers needed a clear path to a human.
- Lesson the video must carry: scoped, measured, supervised AI — **not** unqualified human replacement.

Source: <https://www.entrepreneur.com/business-news/klarna-ceo-reverses-course-by-hiring-more-humans-not-ai/491396>

## 11. Slovenia Phase 1 budget — back-of-envelope from the essay

- Phase 1 cohorts: **~25,000 teachers + ~70,000 secondary and tertiary students + ~35,000 civil servants + 50,000–150,000 SME starter seats**.
- **Total active seats: ~180,000–280,000.**
- **Target seat ceiling: €100 per active user per year.**
- **Phase 1 annual cost: €18–28 million** — comparable to one line item in Slovenia's Digital Decade allocation, smaller than the existing NpUI envelope.

These are **negotiation targets**, not procurement quotes. The video should call them out as such.
