Beam or Be Left Behind: A Slovenian Plan to Skip Digital Transformation and Jump Straight Into AI

In 2025, just 20% of EU enterprises used AI. Slovenia, at ~22%, is on the headline — but the real story is the internal split: 72% of large Slovenian firms use AI vs only 18% of small ones, a ~54-point gap, the widest in the EU (Eurostat + SURS). Meanwhile, the World Economic Forum projects ~60% of workers will need reskilling by 2030 and the OECD puts 28% of employment in occupations at the highest risk of automation.

These two numbers tell one story: capability is now cheap, calibration is scarce. This short documentary lays out a practical 24-month strategy for Slovenia — built around frontier-tool access by September 2026, a Lighthouse Corps of evangelists, a public-sector burden-of-proof flip, and an explicit four-tier confidentiality regime aligned with the EU AI Act.

🎯 The diagnosis — two numbers, one story, one widest-in-the-EU gap.

⚡ The "beaming" moment — why a small metalworking firm in Celje crosses the threshold on a Tuesday morning, and most of Slovenia hasn't.

🛠 Six concrete moves Slovenia can begin before the next budget cycle:
   1. Frontier agentic-system access for every secondary/tertiary student, teacher, civil servant, and SME starter cohort by 1 September 2026.
   2. Flip the public-sector burden of proof (Shopify "Reflexive AI" model).
   3. Lighthouse Corps — 200–300 evangelists, one metric: "first joy moments."
   4. Confidentiality on the platform layer with four explicit tiers.
   5. SME 99% through GZS, OZS, Eurochambres and AmCham — vouchers for outcomes not hours.
   6. Re-anchor the public story: cognitive expansion, not job-loss event.

Phase 1 back-of-envelope budget: ~180–280k seats × €100/year ceiling = **€18–28M/year** — smaller than one line in Slovenia's Digital Decade allocation.
