# Pike's Rules for the Agentic Age: Why 1989 Programming Wisdom Is Your Best AI Strategy

In 1989, Rob Pike wrote five rules of programming at Bell Labs. In 2026, they're the best playbook for building reliable AI agent systems.

🧠 Five rules, five problems. Each of Pike's principles — don't guess at bottlenecks, measure first, don't get fancy, simple is less buggy, data dominates — maps directly to a production challenge in agentic engineering. Context compression, codebase instrumentation, linting, multi-agent coordination, and specification fatigue all have 37-year-old solutions.

⚡ 2025 was the year of manufactured complexity. Anthropic and OpenAI partnered with Accenture and Deloitte because their enterprise customers couldn't use what they shipped. Meanwhile, the teams actually shipping reliable agents kept rediscovering: the old rules still work. Factory.ai's data shows the agent isn't broken — the environment is.

🔧 A five-phase practical framework. Foundation (fix the environment first), Simplicity (one agent, one job), Measurement (baseline before optimizing), Data Structures (invest in specs, not context dumps), and Scale (only when measurement demands it). With key questions for each phase and a cheat sheet you can pin to your wall.
