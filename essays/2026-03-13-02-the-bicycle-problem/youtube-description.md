The Bicycle Problem: Why Going Slower Won't Save Your Career in the Age of AI

Going slower on a bicycle doesn't make you safer -- it makes balancing harder. The same principle now governs your career. If you're "waiting for AI to mature," you're not being cautious. You're wobbling.

This isn't about which tools to learn. It's about the psychological rewiring that separates people who will thrive from people who will spend three years wondering what happened.

🧠 The three psychological walls -- Identity Threat (feeling like a beginner in your own field), the Permission Gap (80% quit in 3 weeks because nobody taught the judgment layer), and the Comfort of Familiar Struggle (8 hours of manual work feels productive; 10 minutes with AI feels like cheating)

🎯 The six skills that actually matter -- context assembly, quality judgment, task decomposition, iterative refinement, frontier recognition, and rejection competency. None are technical. All are about judgment.

📈 The compounding gap -- someone who started 6 months ago has 6 months of calibration you can't shortcut. Entry-level hiring down 67%. The workforce is bifurcating. Every quarter of delay creates exponentially more distance.
