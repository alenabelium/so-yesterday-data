# New open-source "Nano Banana" is here! INSANELY fast

## Executive Summary

Flux 2 Klein is a new open-source image generator and editor that combines both capabilities in a single model, functioning as an open-source alternative to Nano Banana. Available in 9B and 4B parameter variants, the 4B distilled version generates images in about 2 seconds and can run with as little as 2 GB of VRAM using GGUF quantizations. Head-to-head tests against Qwen Image 2512 and Z-Image Turbo show competitive results -- Flux 2 Klein excels at artistic styles and image editing consistency but lags in text rendering and anatomy. The 4B model uses the permissive Apache 2 license, making it suitable for commercial use.

## Key Points

- Flux 2 Klein combines image generation and editing in one model, unlike competitors where you need separate models (e.g., Qwen Image for generation and Qwen ImageEdit for editing), saving significant disk space and simplifying workflows. [01:05](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2OrOufa3eoc&t=65)
- In image editing tests, Flux 2 Klein outperformed Qwen ImageEdit 2511 at preserving original photo colors and consistency, such as correctly maintaining a character's sunglasses and accessories when changing the camera perspective from a complex barbecue scene. [11:23](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2OrOufa3eoc&t=683)
- GGUF quantized versions as small as 1.8 GB make Flux 2 Klein runnable on GPUs with just 2 GB of VRAM or even on CPU/AMD devices, and the ComfyUI workflow generates images in roughly 2-4 seconds on 16 GB VRAM. [28:23](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2OrOufa3eoc&t=1703)
