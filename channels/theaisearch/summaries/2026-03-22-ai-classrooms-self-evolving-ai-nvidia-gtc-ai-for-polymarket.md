# AI classrooms, self-evolving AI, Nvidia GTC, AI for polymarket, Google app builder: AI NEWS

## Executive Summary

This packed AI news roundup covers Minimax M2.7, the first model claimed to deeply participate in its own evolution through recursive self-improvement during training; Xiaomi's Mimo V2 Pro, a trillion-parameter mixture-of-experts model rivaling Opus 4.6 on agentic benchmarks; OpenMIC, an open-source platform that generates interactive virtual classrooms with AI teachers and classmates; MetaClaw, a framework that makes AI agents continuously learn from conversations; and Google's Spark VSSR video upscaler. Other highlights include DreamVerse for near-real-time video generation, Google's app builder for vibe coding, Glyph Printer for accurate multilingual text rendering in images, and humanoid robots playing tennis.

## Key Points

- Minimax M2.7 claims to be the first model that "deeply participated in its own evolution" during training, using recursive self-improvement where the model ran experiments and updated its own tools -- raising the possibility of taking humans out of the loop for exponential progress. [02:34](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HCVkBC1Vhcw&t=154)
- OpenMIC creates interactive virtual AI classrooms from a single topic prompt, generating slides, quizzes, interactive simulations, project-based learning, and AI classmates you can chat with -- a free open-source tool that could replace tutoring for many learning scenarios. [08:22](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HCVkBC1Vhcw&t=502)
- MetaClaw is a framework layered on top of OpenClaw that turns every conversation into a learning signal, automatically extracting skills into a library and optionally fine-tuning the agent during idle time -- enabling AI agents to continuously improve through regular use. [10:29](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HCVkBC1Vhcw&t=629)
