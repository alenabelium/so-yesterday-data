# AI thought-to-text, Qwen 3.5, Lyria 3, realtime videos, 4D worlds, realtime TTS: AI NEWS

## Executive Summary

This AI news roundup covers a packed week of releases including Alibaba's Qwen 3.5, a 397-billion-parameter open-source multimodal model rivaling top closed models in reasoning, coding, and vision tasks. Google drops Gemini 3.1 Pro and Lyria 3, a free music generator available on the Gemini platform. Other highlights include Anchorwave, an open-source interactive 3D world generator; Kitten TTS, an ultra-lightweight text-to-speech model under 25 MB; a Talis chip achieving 17,000 tokens per second by hardcoding Llama 3.1 into hardware; Zuna, an open-source brain-wave-to-text foundation model; and Monarch RT, enabling real-time video generation on a single consumer GPU.

## Key Points

- Alibaba's Qwen 3.5 is a 397B-parameter multimodal model (only 17B active via mixture-of-experts) with a million-token context window, excelling at reasoning, coding, and video understanding while being fully open-sourced. [00:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fnMAIa2PEAk&t=0)
- Talis unveiled a chip that hardcodes Llama 3.1 directly into silicon, achieving 17,000 tokens per second -- 40x faster than Nvidia's B200 -- while costing 20x less to build and consuming 10x less power, pointing toward a future of model-specific AI hardware. [10:03](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fnMAIa2PEAk&t=603)
- Google released Lyria 3, a free AI music generator on the Gemini platform that can compose full songs from text prompts or uploaded images in multiple styles and languages, alongside the incremental but powerful Gemini 3.1 Pro model update. [31:13](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fnMAIa2PEAk&t=1873)
