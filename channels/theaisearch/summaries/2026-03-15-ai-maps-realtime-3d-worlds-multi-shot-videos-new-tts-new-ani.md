# AI maps, realtime 3D worlds, multi-shot videos, new TTS, new anime model: AI NEWS

## Executive Summary

This AI news roundup covers Google Maps gaining AI-powered features including natural language search and immersive 3D navigation; InSpaceHow World FM, a real-time interactive 3D world generator running on a consumer RTX 4090; Nvidia's Neotron 3 Super, a 120B-parameter open-source agentic model with a million-token context window; and Shotverse, a multi-shot cinematic video generator. Additional highlights include MattAnyone 2 for precise video segmentation, a new state-of-the-art anime image model, two powerful open-source TTS generators (Tada and Fish Audio S2), and ComfyUI's new app mode that simplifies complex AI workflows into clean interfaces.

## Key Points

- Google Maps added AI-powered "Ask Maps" for natural language queries that summarize reviews and plan itineraries, plus immersive 3D navigation showing buildings, lane guidance on highways, and nearby parking details -- rolling out across the US. [16:35](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qWzo3ws0uWU&t=995)
- InSpaceHow World FM generates interactive 3D worlds from a single photo or text prompt in real time on a consumer RTX 4090, with long-term scene memory that maintains consistency even when you look away and return -- a significant step toward accessible real-time world generation. [04:52](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qWzo3ws0uWU&t=292)
- Two new open-source TTS models stand out: Tada achieves zero hallucination rate with natural-sounding voice cloning faster than all competitors, while Fish Audio S2 Pro lets users embed tags like pauses, emphasis, laughter, and whispering directly in transcripts for fine-grained speech control.
