# Opus 4.6, GPT 5.3 Codex, StepFun, Qwen3 Coder, new deepfake AIs, new video tools: AI NEWS

## Executive Summary

This was a landmark week for AI model releases. Anthropic launched Claude Opus 4.6, which tops independent leaderboards (LM Arena, Artificial Analysis) and scored 68.8% on ARC-AGI-2, far above competitors. Minutes later, OpenAI released GPT 5.3 Codex -- the first model instrumental in creating itself through recursive self-improvement -- which dominates agentic coding benchmarks like SWE-Bench Pro and Terminal Bench 2. On the open-source side, Step 3.5 Flash (196B MoE, 11B active) matches top closed models while being drastically cheaper, MiniCPM-o4.5 offers a 9B omnimodal model with real-time voice and video interaction, and Qwen 3 Coder Next provides state-of-the-art agentic coding at just 80B parameters. ZAI also released GLM OCR, which outperforms all open and closed source competitors.

## Key Points

- GPT 5.3 Codex is the first model that was instrumental in creating itself -- the Codex team used early versions to debug its own training, manage deployment, and diagnose evaluations, representing recursive self-improvement. It scores 56.8% on SWE-Bench Pro, far above Opus 4.5's 45.89%, and leads Terminal Bench 2 by a large margin. [09:43](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-D7o3E0eBf4&t=583)
- Claude Opus 4.6 scored 68.8% on ARC-AGI-2, demonstrating a remarkable ability to learn new patterns never seen in training data, while ranking #1 on both LM Arena and Artificial Analysis independent leaderboards above GPT 5.2 and Gemini 3 Pro. [06:47](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-D7o3E0eBf4&t=407)
- MiniCPM-o4.5 is an open-source 9B parameter omnimodal model that enables real-time voice and video interaction, voice cloning (e.g., imitating Elon Musk), OCR, and visual reasoning, outperforming all other omni models while fitting comfortably on a consumer 4090 GPU at just 23.4 GB. [16:50](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-D7o3E0eBf4&t=1010)
