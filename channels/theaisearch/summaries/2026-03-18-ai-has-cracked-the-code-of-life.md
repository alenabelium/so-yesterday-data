# AI has cracked the code of life

## Executive Summary

Researchers published Evo 2 in Nature, a biological foundation model trained on 9 trillion DNA base pairs across the entire spectrum of life, with a million-token context window at single-nucleotide resolution. Without ever being taught biology labels, Evo 2 learned to identify harmful mutations, detect cancer-linked BRCA gene variants, and understand exceptions like the ciliate genetic code -- all through zero-shot prediction by recognizing evolutionary patterns. Most remarkably, it can generate complete functional genomes from scratch, producing viable DNA for human mitochondria, bacteria, and yeast that was validated by external tools including AlphaFold 3. The model is open-sourced but intentionally excludes human virus DNA from its training data as a biosecurity safeguard.

## Key Points

- Evo 2 correctly identifies cancer-linked BRCA gene mutations through zero-shot prediction -- without ever being trained on medical data -- by recognizing evolutionary patterns in DNA sequences across 9 trillion base pairs from all domains of life. [14:51](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAq1O-tEVsE&t=891)
- The model generated complete functional genomes from scratch for human mitochondria (16,000 letters), a bacterium (580,000 letters), and yeast, with external tools including AlphaFold 3 confirming that the generated proteins fold correctly and interlock as they would in real organisms. [17:51](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAq1O-tEVsE&t=1071)
- As a biosecurity measure, all eukaryotic virus DNA was excluded from training data; when tested, the model showed high perplexity (confusion) on virus sequences and generated only gibberish when prompted to create dangerous pathogens, though open-sourcing the training code still raises ethical concerns. [23:01](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAq1O-tEVsE&t=1381)
