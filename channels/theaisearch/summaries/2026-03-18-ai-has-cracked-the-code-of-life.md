# AI has cracked the code of life

## Executive Summary

Researchers have developed Evo 2, a biological foundation model trained on 9 trillion DNA base pairs that functions like a large language model but for the genetic code. By utilizing a massive context window, the AI can understand complex regulatory elements, predict the effects of mutations without explicit labels, and generate functional genomes for organisms ranging from mitochondria to yeast. While the model includes safety filters to prevent the generation of dangerous human viruses, its open-source release raises significant ethical questions regarding biosecurity and the potential for engineering new life forms.

## Key Points

- Evo 2 leverages a one-million token context window to analyze long-range DNA interactions, allowing it to identify critical regulatory sequences and predict mutation viability through zero-shot learning [00:03:58](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAq1O-tEVsE&t=238).
- The model successfully generated complete, functional genomes for human mitochondria, bacteria, and yeast, with external validation confirming the biological viability of the synthesized proteins [00:17:51](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAq1O-tEVsE&t=1071).
- To mitigate biosecurity risks, the training data intentionally excluded eukaryotic viruses, causing the model to fail when attempting to generate dangerous pathogens [00:23:01](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAq1O-tEVsE&t=1381). [MM:SS](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAq1O-tEVsE&t=SECONDS)
