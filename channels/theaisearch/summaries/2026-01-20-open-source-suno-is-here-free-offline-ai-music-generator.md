# Open-source SUNO is here! Free offline AI music generator

## Executive Summary

The video introduces Heart Moola, a new open-source AI music generator that rivals Suno v5 in quality while offering the freedom of offline, unlimited local use. It highlights how recent lawsuits have forced commercial platforms like Suno and Udio to impose strict licensing and download restrictions, making open-source alternatives essential for creators. The presenter demonstrates the model's capabilities across various genres and languages before providing a comprehensive step-by-step guide to installing and running the software on a personal computer.

## Key Points

- Heart Moola offers production-level music generation comparable to Suno v5 but operates locally, bypassing the licensing restrictions and download bans currently affecting major commercial AI music platforms [00:02:27](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=54YB-hjZDR4&t=147).
- To run the model, users must install Git and Miniconda, create a Python 3.10 virtual environment, and download approximately 16GB of model weights from Hugging Face, requiring significant VRAM [00:15:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=54YB-hjZDR4&t=946).
- The software is released under the permissive Apache 2.0 license, allowing users to generate songs for commercial purposes without the legal hurdles associated with proprietary AI music tools [00:33:01](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=54YB-hjZDR4&t=1981).
- Generation is controlled via command-line parameters where users can fine-tune output by adjusting settings like 'cfg_scale' for prompt adherence and 'temperature' for creativity [00:29:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=54YB-hjZDR4&t=1740) [MM:SS](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=54YB-hjZDR4&t=SECONDS)
