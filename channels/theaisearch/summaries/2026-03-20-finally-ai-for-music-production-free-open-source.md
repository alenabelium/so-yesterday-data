# Finally, AI for music production! Free & open source

## Executive Summary

This video introduces Foundation One, a free and open-source AI model capable of generating musically coherent audio stems that strictly adhere to user-specified tempo, key, and bar counts. The presenter demonstrates how to use the tool to create individual instrument tracks, export MIDI files for further editing, and layer these elements in a DAW to compose full songs. A detailed tutorial covers the local installation process using Git and Conda, highlighting the model's efficiency on consumer GPUs with as little as 8GB of VRAM.

## Key Points

- Foundation One generates high-quality, musically coherent loops that strictly follow specified BPM, key, and bar counts, allowing producers to layer stems into a cohesive song [00:00:52](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CMuwP0u-Sjg&t=52).
- Users can export generated audio as MIDI files to swap instruments or modify melodies within their DAW, offering significant flexibility for post-production editing [08:09](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CMuwP0u-Sjg&t=489).
- The model runs locally on consumer GPUs with at least 8GB of VRAM, requiring a setup involving Git, Miniconda, and specific Python dependencies for offline, unlimited generation [16:39](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CMuwP0u-Sjg&t=999).
- Style transfer features allow users to upload a reference audio clip and apply new instruments or effects while retaining the original melody and structure [29:17](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CMuwP0u-Sjg&t=1757) [MM:SS](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CMuwP0u-Sjg&t=SECONDS)
