# Finally, AI for music production! Free & open source

## Executive Summary

Foundation One is a free, open-source AI model purpose-built for music production that generates musically coherent loops following specific tempo (BPM), key, bar count, instrument timbre, and effects like reverb, delay, and distortion. Unlike full-song generators, it creates individual stems -- bass lines, synth leads, drums, strings -- that producers can layer and mix in a DAW to build complete tracks. The model runs locally, generates a track in about 15 seconds on a 16 GB VRAM GPU, and works with low VRAM setups. The video demonstrates building a multi-track song from scratch by generating separate stems and layering them in a digital audio workstation.

## Key Points

- Foundation One understands professional music production terminology including BPM, bar count, key signatures, instrument types, synth wave shapes, and effects like phaser, reverb, delay, and distortion -- producing stems that accurately follow all specified parameters. - The tutorial demonstrates a practical production workflow: generating separate stems (synth lead, piano, bass, drums) at matching BPM and key, then layering them in a DAW to create a cohesive track -- each stem taking only about 15 seconds to generate locally. - Unlike AI music generators that produce finished songs, Foundation One is designed for production workflows where musicians need individual, mixable stems -- giving producers fine-grained control over each instrument layer rather than an opaque all-in-one output.
