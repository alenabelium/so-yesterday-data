# Google’s open-source AI, Claude Code leaked, new Wan, new Qwen, image gen on phone: AI NEWS

## Executive Summary

This packed AI news roundup covers Google's Gemma 4 release, a family of open-source multimodal models (2B to 31B parameters) that run on phones and consumer hardware while rivaling much larger models in performance. Other major releases include Netflix's VOID for object deletion in video, GenSearcher for web-grounded image generation, LongCat Audio DiT for voice cloning, SeeThrough for anime image decomposition into animatable layers, and a hybrid memory framework for world models that maintains scene consistency. Alibaba dropped new Qwen models and video/image generators, while ZAI released a state-of-the-art vision model.

## Key Points

- Google's Gemma 4 is a fully open-source (Apache 2) multimodal model family supporting text, images, and audio natively, with the 24B model nearly matching Kimi K2.5 (which is 35x larger) in performance -- bringing frontier-class AI to phones and consumer hardware. [00:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o5rGuknRw2A&t=0)
- LongCat Audio DiT from Meituan is a new text-to-speech generator and voice cloner that ranks among the best for tone accuracy and naturalness, with models ranging from 6 GB to 15.3 GB that fit on consumer GPUs and generate cloned speech in under 10 seconds. [12:53](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o5rGuknRw2A&t=773)
- The Hybrid Memory (Hydra) framework for world models compresses scenes into memory tokens and actively searches them during generation, allowing AI-generated 3D worlds to maintain object persistence even when the camera pans away and returns.
