# Realtime AI waifus, Qwen 3.5, persistent memory, multiplayer gameplay, new image models: AI NEWS

## Executive Summary

This weekly AI news roundup covers advances in video reasoning, 3D reconstruction, persistent memory, multiplayer AI, and real-time avatars. Key highlights include VBVR, a reasoning framework that enables video generators to solve visual puzzles; Solaris, which generates synchronized Minecraft gameplay from two player perspectives simultaneously; Sakana AI's doc-to-LoRA and text-to-LoRA methods for compressing documents and instructions into persistent memory adapters; and Sarah, a real-time full-body VR avatar system. Alibaba also released smaller variants of Qwen 3.5 that fit on consumer GPUs, while PhysicEdit introduced physics-aware image editing surpassing Nano Banana in physical accuracy.

## Key Points

- Sakana AI introduced doc-to-LoRA and text-to-LoRA, methods that compress documents or text instructions into lightweight LoRA adapters, giving LLMs a form of persistent memory that survives long conversations without re-pasting context. [30:06](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8grIT-xK50M&t=1806)
- Solaris generates synchronized first-person Minecraft gameplay videos from two players' perspectives simultaneously, representing a step toward training multi-agent autonomous AI systems that can interact in shared environments. [11:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8grIT-xK50M&t=690)
- Sarah is a real-time VR avatar system that generates full-body movements with natural eye contact and dynamic gestures, running fast enough for live VR headsets and pointing toward a future of immersive AI companions. [39:34](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8grIT-xK50M&t=2374)
