# KLING 3.0 is crazy...

## Executive Summary

Kling 3.0 represents a major upgrade in AI video generation, introducing a multi-shot feature that allows users to define individual shots with separate prompts, durations, and camera directions, then stitch them into coherent 15-second cinematic sequences with consistent characters. The new Video 3 Omni model functions as a "Nano Banana for video," enabling natural-language video editing with reference images, character swapping, and style changes while now supporting native audio and multi-shot. Testing shows significant improvements in physical realism (breakdancing, fight scenes), multilingual dialogue including Japanese and Cantonese, and expressive character animations, though fine details in fast-moving scenes still have occasional flaws.

## Key Points

- The multi-shot feature is the standout addition, allowing users to define up to 15 seconds of video split across custom shots with individual prompts, durations, and camera directions, while maintaining exceptional character consistency across all shots in the scene. [01:29](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_ey0HNGdMBk&t=89)
- Video 3 Omni enables Nano Banana-style video editing using reference images and natural language -- combining a reference car, dress, and person into a coherent video while accurately preserving complex design details, and supporting iterative edits like changing outfits or car colors on existing generations. [09:21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_ey0HNGdMBk&t=561)
- Kling 3.0 produces the best breakdancing and fight scene generations seen from any video model, with physically accurate movements and camera control that follows prompt instructions precisely, though fine details like anatomy and sword rendering still have occasional errors in fast-moving sequences.
