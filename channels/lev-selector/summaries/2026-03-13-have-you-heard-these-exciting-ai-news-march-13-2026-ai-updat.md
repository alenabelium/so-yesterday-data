# Have you heard these exciting AI news? - March 13, 2026 AI Updates Weekly

## Executive Summary

The presenter declares "winner: Cline + Claude Code" after switching from Cline to Claude Code and experiencing dramatically better results with the same Opus 4.6 model, attributing it to Anthropic's deep co-tuning of Claude Code with the Claude model over the past year. Open Claw reached 310,000 GitHub stars as Nvidia launched NemoClaw and the ecosystem of "awesome claws" grew past 20 variants. Major developments include Yann LeCun's AMI Labs raising over $1 billion from Jeff Bezos and Nvidia, Databricks' CARL system outperforming larger models through reinforcement learning on synthetic problems, and Anthropic abandoning its safety-first pledge due to competitive pressure. The Alibaba Qwen team restructuring threatens the world's most downloaded open-source model family.

## Key Points

- Switching from Cline to Claude Code with the same Opus 4.6 model produced dramatically better results because Anthropic has deeply co-tuned Claude Code's tooling with the Claude model over the past year -- the presenter describes it as "talking to a genius versus an average programmer" despite using the identical underlying model. - Anthropic abandoned its founding safety-first pledge, with chief science officer Jared Kaplan stating "the pledge doesn't work if competitors are racing ahead," shifting from "we won't build it unless it's safe" to "we'll beat it as safely as the competitor does" -- a fundamental change in the company's identity. - Alibaba's Qwen team -- creators of the world's most downloaded open-source model family -- lost its tech lead and key contributors after corporate restructuring fragmented the team under Alibaba Cloud's commercial priorities; if Qwen stops open-source output, it will leave a massive gap no other company can currently fill.
