# Have you heard these exciting AI news? - February 27, 2026 AI Updates Weekly

## Executive Summary

This episode emphasizes that harness engineering -- the infrastructure around models -- matters more than the models themselves, with top frontier models completing only 24% of real-world tasks despite 90%+ benchmark scores. Open Claw now has 232,000+ GitHub stars and its ecosystem continues to expand with alternatives like Zero Claw (Rust, edge devices) and Iron Claw. Chinese AI firms were caught running industrial-scale distillation of Claude models through 16 million queries and 24,000 fraudulent accounts. Practical highlights include a chip that hardcodes LLM weights directly into silicon for 17,000 tokens/second inference, Lime Otley's no-code AI agency model proving successful for enterprise clients, and Open Claw's Zapier MCP integration giving agents access to 8,000 applications.

## Key Points

- A new chip hardcodes LLM weights (Llama 3.1 8B) directly into silicon, eliminating the need for CPU, GPU, or external memory and achieving approximately 17,000 tokens per second -- ideal for edge devices, manufacturing, and automobiles, with new models taking 2-3 months to produce as chips. [07:33](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d5sHAgQzzo0&t=453)
- Chinese firms DeepSeek, Moonshot, and MiniMax ran industrial-scale distillation of Anthropic's Claude models using 16 million queries through roughly 24,000 fraudulent accounts, though experts note this volume is insufficient for full model training and was likely used for tuning or benchmarking. [09:01](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d5sHAgQzzo0&t=541)
- Open Claw's Zapier MCP integration gives agents access to 8,000 applications including Slack, Gmail, Linear, and Notion; combined with three-layer memory solutions (daily notes, core memory files, semantic search with vector database), users can configure increasingly sophisticated autonomous workflows. [16:25](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d5sHAgQzzo0&t=985)
