# Have you heard these exciting AI news? - January 02, 2026 AI Updates Weekly

## Executive Summary

This weekly AI update covers the growing acceptance of shipping AI-generated code without line-by-line review, citing Andrej Karpathy and Anthropic engineers who now produce hundreds of PRs per week entirely written by Claude. Meta's $2 billion acquisition of Manus highlights the rise of agent companies versus foundational LLM providers. The episode also covers VL-JUPA, Yann LeCun's breakthrough model that operates in embedding space rather than token space, achieving strong results with only 1.6 billion parameters, and discusses the erosion of Google's search monopoly as AI-powered alternatives gain traction.

## Key Points

- Top programmers now embrace vibe coding -- Anthropic's Boris Churnney reports 100% of Claude code is written by Claude itself, landing 259 PRs and 40,000 lines in 30 days, shifting the developer role from writing code to orchestrating AI agents. [04:55](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=n01-OaEiYzA&t=295)
- VL-JUPA from Yann LeCun's team predicts meaning through continuous embeddings rather than text tokens, matching models trained on 86 billion samples with only 1.6 billion parameters and 2 billion training samples -- a potential post-LLM paradigm for robotics and real-time video understanding. [07:40](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=n01-OaEiYzA&t=460)
- Google's search market share dropped below 90% as ChatGPT and Perplexity grow aggressively, with 60% of Google searches now ending without a website click, forcing businesses to optimize for AI citations rather than traditional SEO rankings.
