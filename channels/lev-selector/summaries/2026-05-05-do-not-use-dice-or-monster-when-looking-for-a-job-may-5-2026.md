# Do Not use Dice or Monster when looking for a Job - May 5, 2026

## Executive Summary

This video warns job seekers against using legacy platforms like Dice and Monster due to their current prevalence of low-quality listings and significant security risks. The speaker highlights that these sites are now dominated by offshore staffing firms that scrape personal data, often leading to identity theft and exposure to fake job postings. Instead of relying on these dangerous aggregators, the presentation advocates for safer, more effective channels such as LinkedIn, Hacker News, and direct company career pages. The core argument is that prioritizing direct relationships and reputable sources is essential for a secure and successful job search in the current landscape.

## Key Points

- Dice and Monster are now ineffective and dangerous due to being populated by offshore firms that scrape resumes and demand sensitive personal data like social security numbers [00:00:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0wApDvhL6Gg&t=0).
- Many listings on these platforms are fake or low-paying, serving primarily as lead generation tools that expose users to spam and identity theft risks [00:00:15](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0wApDvhL6Gg&t=15).
- Job seekers should prioritize safer alternatives like LinkedIn, Hacker News, and direct company career pages while remaining skeptical of vague inbound communications [00:00:45](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0wApDvhL6Gg&t=45).
