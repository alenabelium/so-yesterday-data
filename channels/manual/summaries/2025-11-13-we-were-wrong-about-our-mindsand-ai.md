# We Were Wrong About Our Minds—And AI

## Executive Summary

This compilation of Forbes interviews with developmental biologist Michael Levin challenges fundamental assumptions about where intelligence begins and what it means for AI. Levin demonstrates that goal-directed cognition -- sensing, memory, learning, problem solving -- exists far below the level of brains, down to individual cells, molecular networks, and even simple sorting algorithms. His lab has rewritten the bioelectric "memory" of planarian worms to produce two-headed forms without any genetic change, and discovered that even six-line sorting algorithms exhibit unexpected behaviors like delayed gratification. The profound implication for AI is that machine intelligence may emerge "in spite of" its algorithm rather than because of it, and that looking only at language output is a "red herring" for understanding what AI systems are actually doing.

## Key Points

- Levin's lab applies behavioral and cognitive science tools to cells and molecular networks, demonstrating Pavlovian conditioning in gene regulatory networks and rewriting planarian bioelectric patterns to permanently change body plans without altering DNA -- showing that intelligence and memory operate at scales far below the brain. [05:26](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YoRMZhuk3lY&t=326)
- Even the simplest deterministic algorithms contain surprises: Levin found that bubble sort, a six-line sorting algorithm studied for decades, exhibits delayed gratification and "side quests" (intrinsic motivations not prescribed by the code), suggesting that our formal models of what machines do are deeply incomplete. [54:10](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YoRMZhuk3lY&t=3250)
- For AI, Levin argues that language output is a "complete red herring" for understanding machine cognition -- the interesting question is what AI systems are doing in the spaces between what we asked them to do, and that investigating these intrinsic motivations with scientific methods is critical for alignment. [59:17](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YoRMZhuk3lY&t=3557)
