# Why Owning a Car Won't Make Sense, Robotaxi Plans, and Uber’s Trillion $ Market 

## Executive Summary

Uber CEO Dara Khosrowshahi argues that the future of mobility will be a hybrid ecosystem where autonomous robotaxis coexist with human drivers, ultimately making car ownership economically obsolete. He outlines Uber's strategy to act as an aggregator for various autonomous partners and vehicle manufacturers rather than building its own hardware vertically. The discussion highlights how this transition will expand market demand, improve safety, and shift the economic model from labor-intensive driving to capital-based fleet management.

## Key Points

- Uber is positioning itself as a platform aggregator for over 20 autonomous partners, including Waymo and Nvidia, to facilitate a hybrid fleet of human and robot drivers rather than competing vertically like Tesla. [04:09](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fzKVYNBg50E&t=249)
- The company predicts that within 10 years, every new car sold will be autonomous-ready, leading to a market where owning a car is no longer financially sensible compared to on-demand robotaxi services. [16:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fzKVYNBg50E&t=990)
- To manage the transition for human drivers, Uber plans to slow new driver recruitment in autonomous markets while enabling existing drivers to become fleet owners who manage their own small fleets of robotaxis. [24:35](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fzKVYNBg50E&t=1475)
