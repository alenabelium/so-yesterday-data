# Cathie Wood: “AI and Bitcoin Are Moving Faster Than We Expected” 

## Executive Summary

Cathie Wood, founder of ARK Invest, joins the Moonshots team to walk through ARK's 2026 Big Ideas report, covering projected GDP acceleration, inference cost collapse, orbital data centers, robotaxi economics, the US-China AI race, and the Claudebot/Moltbot phenomenon. Wood argues that five converging technology platforms (robotics, energy storage, AI, blockchain, and multiomic sequencing) will drive global real GDP growth to 7%+ annually, a conservative estimate that would represent a 2.5x step-function increase over the prior 125 years. She frames Wright's Law as the key economic principle, where every cumulative doubling of units produced drives consistent cost declines, and argues that most financial institutions are structurally unable to see this because they organize research by siloed sectors rather than cross-cutting technologies.

## Key Points

- ARK projects 7%+ global real GDP growth driven by five converging technology platforms, with inference costs collapsing 99% per year; Wood argues this is conservative and that traditional economists miss it because they are trapped in 125 years of 3% growth history and sector-siloed research models. [07:43](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VoW13oUTTV0&t=463)
- The robotaxi opportunity could replace 400 million privately owned US cars with just 24 million autonomous vehicles, while orbital data centers become economically viable as SpaceX launch costs drop below $100/kg, with solar panels being 6x more efficient in space than on Earth. [15:05](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VoW13oUTTV0&t=905)
- China is stealing the march on open-source AI after US software export restrictions forced them into the open-source movement, with the Claudebot (now Moltbot) explosion demonstrating how open-source personal AI agents are already reshaping productivity; Wood expects inflation to drop below 2% and go negative as AI-driven productivity gains are systematically undermeasured by current economic statistics.
