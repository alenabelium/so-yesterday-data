# Google Invests $40B Into Anthropic, GPT 5.5 Drops, and Google Cloud Dominates | EP #252

## Executive Summary

Peter Diamandis and co-hosts cover Google's massive $40B investment in Anthropic ($10B cash at $350B valuation plus $30B conditional), Amazon's parallel $33B Anthropic investment, and Google Cloud's dominance with 8th-generation TPUs that are 3x faster in training and designed to run millions of agents in real time. The hosts argue that the AI race is now bottlenecked at TSMC and energy supply rather than model innovation, with Dave noting that only Elon Musk is publicly discussing this constraint. They also discuss how Anthropic's revenue could hit $40-70B by year-end, limited primarily by compute availability rather than demand, and how Google now accounts for roughly a quarter of all AI compute on the planet with TPUs being designed by TPUs in a recursive self-improvement loop.

## Key Points

- Google committed $40B to Anthropic at a $350B valuation -- roughly one-third the trillion-dollar secondary market price -- showing how desperately frontier labs need compute, while Amazon invested $33B with Anthropic committing to spend $100B+ on AWS over the next decade in return. [36:48](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RCbJKkG_mZs&t=2208)
- Google unveiled 8th-generation TPUs (TPU 8T for training, TPU 8i for inference) that are 3x faster with 80% better performance per dollar, and Google now processes over 16 billion tokens per minute with 75% of its code written by AI -- with the TPUs themselves being designed by AI in a recursive self-improvement loop. [30:50](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RCbJKkG_mZs&t=1850)
- The entire AI industry is bottlenecked at TSMC semiconductor fabrication and energy supply, with only three companies (Samsung, Intel, TSMC) capable of making frontier AI chips; Dave argues this is the actual constraint that no one except Elon Musk is willing to discuss publicly. [38:24](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RCbJKkG_mZs&t=2304)
