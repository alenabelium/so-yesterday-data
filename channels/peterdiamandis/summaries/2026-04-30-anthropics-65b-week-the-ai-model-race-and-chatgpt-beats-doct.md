# Anthropic’s $65B Week, The AI Model Race, and ChatGPT Beats Doctors 

## Executive Summary

Peter Diamandis and his Moonshots co-hosts survey an extraordinary 8-week period with 15 major AI model releases, focusing on Kimi K2.6, GPT 5.5, and DeepSeek V4. The panel debates whether the AI race is now fundamentally a compute race rather than a model architecture race, citing OpenAI's Noam Brown arguing that weights may matter less than inference-time reasoning compute. They discuss how Kimi K2.6 -- a trillion-parameter open-weight model trained for just $4.6M -- is closing the gap to within 3 months of US frontier models, while GPT 5.5 shows 1% monthly gains on research-grade math benchmarks, leading Alex to declare "math is cooked." The hosts agree that for average users, it no longer matters much which frontier model you pick -- the real unlock is that any of them can now build software for you in an hour.

## Key Points

- GPT 5.5 shows approximately 1% monthly gains on Frontier Math Tier 4 benchmarks, meaning at the current pace (which will likely accelerate) all professional research-grade math problems could be solved within 4-5 years; the 37-point increase in long-context reasoning and 60% reduction in hallucinations over 5.4 make it a significant release. [24:39](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RCbJKkG_mZs&t=1479)
- Kimi K2.6 is a trillion-parameter open-weight model trained for only $4.6M that benchmarks competitively with Claude Opus 4.6 and runs at 1/30th the cost when self-hosted, though the hosts caution that Chinese open-weight models carry unverifiable code injection risks that US closed models do not. [13:33](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RCbJKkG_mZs&t=813)
- The AI model race is now exclusively US vs. China with no European, UK, Japanese, or Indian models on the frontier leaderboard, and models are now self-improving -- which means late starters face an increasingly insurmountable cold-start problem as recursive self-improvement accelerates the leaders.
