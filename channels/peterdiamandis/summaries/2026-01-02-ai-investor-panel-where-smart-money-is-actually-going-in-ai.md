# AI Investor Panel: Where Smart Money Is Actually Going in AI 

## Executive Summary

Peter Diamandis hosts a panel with Anjney Midha (a16z), Bonnie Chan (Hong Kong Exchange), and David Blondon (Link Exponential Ventures) to discuss how the global AI revolution will be funded. The panelists agree that AI investment demand far exceeds traditional venture capital capacity, with over $1 billion deployed per day in the US alone and projections of $3 billion/day by 2030. A central tension emerges: the vast majority of AI wealth creation is locked inside private capital, while the public is largely excluded from participating. The panel warns that energy constraints, civil unrest from inequality, and speculative peripheral investments (robotics hardware, fusion) pose the biggest risks to sustained AI investment momentum.

## Key Points

- All venture capital is effectively flowing into AI, yet it is still not enough; the capital stack is being rewritten with corporate investors like Nvidia, sovereign funds, and public markets all needed to meet insatiable compute and infrastructure demand. [04:27](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7q3fhBR3z-A&t=267)
- The biggest risks to the AI investment boom are energy supply constraints (permitting and power grid limitations), civil blowback as wealth concentrates among a small group of private investors, and speculative capital flowing into capital-intensive peripheral bets like fusion and robotics that could trigger a dot-com-style confidence collapse. [26:44](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7q3fhBR3z-A&t=1604)
- AI startup success rates from top universities are near 100% for vertical application companies, with valuations scaling from $20-30M to unicorn status in under two years, but institutional investors (pension funds, sovereign wealth funds) remain dangerously underexposed to frontier AI wealth creation. [23:53](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7q3fhBR3z-A&t=1433)
