# The Humanoid Takeover: $50T Market, Figure's Full Body Autonomy, and Robots in Dorms #229

## Executive Summary

Peter Diamandis visits Figure headquarters with Brett Adcock, touring 300,000+ square feet of robotics development and witnessing hundreds of Figure 3 robots in various stages of production and testing. The major announcement is Helix 2, Figure's fully neural-net-driven AI stack that eliminates the last 109,000 lines of C++ code, enabling full-body autonomous control including locomotion, manipulation, and planning. Adcock draws a sharp distinction between Figure's closed-loop neural net autonomy and the teleoperated or open-loop demonstrations common across the industry, noting that no other company has shown more than one minute of continuous neural-net-driven humanoid operation. The conversation covers Figure's path to manufacturing at scale, the $50 trillion humanoid labor market opportunity, and Adcock's new AI lab HARK aimed at building a Jarvis-like general-purpose AI.

## Key Points

- Figure's Helix 2 runs entirely on neural nets with zero C++ code remaining, enabling room-scale autonomy where robots perform kitchen tasks, logistics, and manipulation for hours continuously -- a capability Adcock claims no competitor has demonstrated uncut. [40:23](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S_fXhVT67Uw&t=2423)
- The humanoid robot market represents roughly half of global GDP in human labor (~$50 trillion), and Figure expects to begin placing robots on its own assembly lines in 2026 while scaling commercial deployments, with the industry likely consolidating to far fewer than 10 companies globally. [17:06](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S_fXhVT67Uw&t=1026)
- Adcock founded a new AI lab called HARK focused on building true multimodal systems with persistent memory, physical world interaction, and reasoning capabilities -- designing CAD models from single prompts and aiming to create "synthetic humans at scale" that work both digitally and physically. [59:52](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S_fXhVT67Uw&t=3592)
