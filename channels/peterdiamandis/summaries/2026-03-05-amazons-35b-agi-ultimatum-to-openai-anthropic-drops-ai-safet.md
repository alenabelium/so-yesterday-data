# Amazon's $35B AGI Ultimatum to OpenAI & Anthropic Drops AI Safety 

## Executive Summary

This Moonshots episode covers two major stories: Amazon's contingent $35 billion investment in OpenAI (conditional on going public and achieving AGI), and Anthropic formally dropping its 2023 pledge to not train advanced AI unless safety is guaranteed. The panel dissects Anthropic's shift from "we won't build it unless it's safe" to "we'll be as safe as the competition," arguing this was inevitable given the competitive dynamics where alignment-oriented firms always become capabilities firms. The discussion extends to Anthropic's expanding enterprise agent marketplace with co-work plugins for finance, banking, and HR that are decimating SaaS company valuations, and the panel debates the entrepreneurial opportunity in deploying AI within enterprise firewalls. Dave projects that on Anthropic's current revenue trajectory, its implied valuation could reach a quadrillion dollars.

## Key Points

- Anthropic revised its responsible scaling policy, dropping the pledge to not train advanced AI unless safety is guaranteed; the panel argues this was inevitable since alignment and capabilities are inseparable, and unilateral safety from a single heroic lab was never viable -- safety will emerge from competition and balance of powers.
- Amazon's $35 billion contingent offer to OpenAI (requiring IPO and AGI achievement) signals the financialization of super intelligence; the panel notes Amazon was previously all-in on Anthropic, reflecting how rapidly allegiances shift in the AI race.
- Anthropic's new co-work plugins and enterprise agent marketplace are absurdly simple (often just text files and MCP wrappers) yet are carving $1.5 trillion off SaaS market caps, demonstrating that the moat for traditional software companies has evaporated and the real entrepreneurial opportunity lies in deploying AI within secure enterprise environments.
