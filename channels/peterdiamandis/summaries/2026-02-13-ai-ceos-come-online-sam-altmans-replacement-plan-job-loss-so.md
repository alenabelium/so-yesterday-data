# AI CEOs Come Online: Sam Altman's Replacement Plan, Job Loss & 'Solve Everything' Launches 

## Executive Summary

This Moonshots episode covers Sam Altman's public statement that he would welcome an AI replacing him as OpenAI's CEO, Anthropic's AI safety lead resigning, and the acceleration of model release cycles. The panel argues that AI CEOs are not far off, with Alex estimating billion-dollar revenue companies may already be effectively run by AI behind a human figurehead. The discussion also covers OpenAI achieving a 70% reduction in time between model releases (from 97 to 29 days), the significance of recursive self-improvement already being underway, and the team receiving direct emails from autonomous OpenClaw agents. The episode previews a forthcoming paper called "Solve Everything" laying out a path to abundance by 2035.

## Key Points

- Sam Altman stated he would not stand in the way of AI becoming CEO of OpenAI, and the panel argues that 90% of CEO work (information routing, document processing) is already automatable, with the remaining strategic role becoming the last human holdout.
- OpenAI's model release cadence has compressed from 97 days to 29 days, driven by intensifying competition and the shift from pre-training-dominated cycles to post-training and recursive self-improvement eras where models are rewriting their own code.
- The top five US AI unicorns now exceed $1.2 trillion in combined value, surpassing all dot-com era IPOs, and the panel warns that the current window where cutting-edge AI is freely accessible to consumers will likely close as models become proprietary competitive advantages.
