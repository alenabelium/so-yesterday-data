# Anthropic’s $65B Week, The AI Model Race, and ChatGPT Beats Doctors 

## Executive Summary

Peter Diamandis and his Moonshots co-hosts discuss Anthropic's release of Claude Design (built on Opus 4.7), which immediately hit Figma and Adobe stock prices, raising the question of how many SaaS verticals are just one "unhobbling" of latent model capabilities away from disruption. They debate the looming replacement of entry-level software engineers, with Anthropic's own employees estimating 3-month timelines. The hosts also cover XAI's parameter-count roadmap from Elon Musk, the Breakthrough Prize ceremony gathering tech CEOs and Hollywood stars, and the broader pattern of frontier labs competing directly with vertical software businesses. A key theme is that companies need to be more than just a scaffold around AI models -- they need vertically integrated, proprietary capabilities.

## Key Points

- Claude Design represents an "unhobbling" of capabilities already latent in the model, raising the question of how many existing SaaS verticals -- from Lexus Nexis to Bloomberg Terminal to Workday -- are just one product release away from being disrupted by frontier labs. [09:17](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Bj0i-yvIUQs&t=557)
- Anthropic's own employees estimate that entry-level software engineers and researchers will be replaced within 3 months, and the hosts argue this is the canary in the coal mine for all white-collar jobs, with the traditional career ladder of "get a CS degree, get an internship, work your way up" disappearing. [17:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Bj0i-yvIUQs&t=1050)
- The hosts predict a wave of AI-native startups will leapfrog large enterprises that cannot redesign their human-to-human workflows, with incumbents eventually forced to acquire these startups and reverse-engineer into them -- while geographic adoption speeds vary wildly, from warp-speed San Francisco to "sublinear" Europe. [23:39](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Bj0i-yvIUQs&t=1419)
