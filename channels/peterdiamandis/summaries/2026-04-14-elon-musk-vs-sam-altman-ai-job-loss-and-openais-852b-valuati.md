# Elon Musk vs. Sam Altman, AI Job Loss, and OpenAI’s $852B Valuation 

## Executive Summary

Peter Diamandis and the Moonshots panel cover the intensifying AI landscape: XAI is being rebuilt from the ground up with SpaceX engineers as Elon admits it "was not built right the first time," while training seven models up to 10 trillion parameters on Colossus 2. The Musk vs. Altman $100 billion lawsuit begins jury selection April 27th, with Diamandis predicting a settlement involving Sam stepping down as CEO. The panel debates OpenAI's $852 billion valuation under pressure from Anthropic's surging secondary market demand, the coming era of one-person unicorns (Medv hit $401M ARR with one founder), and Sam Altman's public warning of imminent world-shaking cyber attacks. Global VC investment in AI hit $242 billion in Q1 2026 alone -- $3 billion per day -- with 64% concentrated in just four companies.

## Key Points

- XAI is being rebuilt from the foundations up after Elon admitted the company was not built correctly the first time: eight founding engineers left including three co-founders, SpaceX engineers are filling leadership, and Alex Wissner-Gross suspects the core issue was benchmark-gaming rather than genuine capability, while the company trains models up to 10 trillion parameters on 700,000 GPUs worth $18 billion in hardware. [05:42](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5ak26W2YNRY&t=342)
- The panel highlights the emergence of one-person unicorns as proof that AI has collapsed the minimum viable team: Medv achieved $401 million in ARR with a single founder before hiring his brother, and the average AI unicorn founder age has dropped from 40 to 29 since 2020, with Alex Wissner-Gross arguing that entrepreneurship is transforming into simply overseeing AI operators -- something people of all temperaments can do. [01:34:07](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5ak26W2YNRY&t=5647)
- Anthropic's secondary market demand is 3x higher than OpenAI's ($2B vs $600M), with investors pricing Anthropic at $600B while OpenAI trades at a 10% discount to its last round -- reflecting enterprise trust in Claude's reliability and the broader market realization that one company focused on the smartest AI at all costs may be inventing the new playbook where users switch to whoever has the best intelligence, regardless of installed base.
