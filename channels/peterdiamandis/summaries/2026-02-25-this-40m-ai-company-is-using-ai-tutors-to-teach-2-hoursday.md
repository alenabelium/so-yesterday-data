# This $40M AI Company Is Using AI Tutors to Teach 2 Hours/Day 

## Executive Summary

Peter Diamandis interviews Mackenzie Price (CEO) and Joe Lamont (principal and funder) of Alpha Schools, a K-12 education company that delivers a full academic program in just two hours per day using AI tutors grounded in 40 years of learning science research. Alpha School students achieve average SAT scores of 1410 across all high schoolers (1535 for seniors), placing them in the top percentiles nationally, while spending their remaining school hours on life skills workshops including entrepreneurship, public speaking, and passion projects. The conversation highlights the crisis in American education -- only 22% of high school seniors are math proficient, college is perceived as less important by 65% of Americans, and college graduates face the longest unemployment durations. Alpha Schools has invested over $100 million in its learning platform and pays guides six-figure salaries, fundamentally reimagining the role of adults in education from lecturers to motivational mentors.

## Key Points

- Alpha Schools' AI-tutor-driven model achieves 10x faster learning by applying mastery-based, personalized instruction that keeps students in the zone of proximal development, a method learning scientists have validated for 40 years but that never worked in the traditional teacher-in-front-of-classroom model.
- The school is built around five pillars: kids must love school (90%+ do, with many preferring school to vacation), 10x faster learning, life skills development in the afternoon, transforming teachers into motivational guides paid six-figure salaries, and a strong character-focused culture.
- With $40M+ raised and over 100 million invested in the Timeback learning platform, Alpha Schools received 80,000 applicants for guide roles and is scaling nationally, demonstrating that AI-driven education can dramatically outperform traditional schooling while freeing students to pursue passion projects and real-world entrepreneurship.
