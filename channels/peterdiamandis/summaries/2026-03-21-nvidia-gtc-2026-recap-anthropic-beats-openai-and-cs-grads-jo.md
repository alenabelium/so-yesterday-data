# NVIDIA GTC 2026 Recap, Anthropic Beats OpenAI, and CS Grads Job Decline 

## Executive Summary

Peter Diamandis and the Moonshot mates break down the biggest tech developments of the week, headlined by Nvidia's GTC 2026 where Jensen Huang projected $1 trillion in revenue bookings through 2027 and announced Nemo Claw to bring OpenClaw into enterprises. Anthropic is dominating the enterprise AI market, surging from 40% to 73% of first-time enterprise customers in just three months while OpenAI scales back its Stargate data center plans. The panel also examines the collapse of CS graduate job placements -- dropping from 89% to just 19% over two years -- arguing that entrepreneurship and getting on cap tables is the only viable career path forward. Throughout, the hosts emphasize that inference-time compute is driving a 1,000x cost reduction in AI capabilities, and that the organizational singularity enabled by agentic AI will fundamentally restructure every company and institution.

## Key Points

- Nvidia's GTC 2026 showcased Jensen Huang's vision to power everything from robotics to orbital data centers, with $1 trillion in revenue bookings projected through 2027 and the announcement of Nemo Claw to bring OpenClaw into enterprise environments securely. [06:12](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uOGHXAfvK8w&t=372)
- Anthropic has surged to 73% market share among first-time enterprise customers, up from 40% in just three months, while OpenAI dropped to 26% -- a shift the panel attributes to Anthropic's focused enterprise strategy and partnership-friendly approach versus OpenAI's overextension into hardware, devices, and consumer markets. [46:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uOGHXAfvK8w&t=2791)
- Computer science graduate job placements have collapsed from 89% to 19% over two years with starting salaries falling below $61,000, signaling a broader shift where the panel argues the only viable career path is entrepreneurship, joining startups, and getting equity in AI-driven companies.
