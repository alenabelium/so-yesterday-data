# David Sinclair on the Longevity Pill, Age Reversal Timelines, and Updated Protocols 

## Executive Summary

David Sinclair discusses the imminent human trials of epigenetic reprogramming using a subset of Yamanaka genes (OSK) to reverse aging and cure conditions like blindness, with plans to expand to whole-body treatments. He contrasts expensive viral gene therapies with a promising, low-cost small-molecule 'longevity pill' developed using AI to screen billions of compounds. Sinclair emphasizes that while initial access may be limited, the goal is to make these life-extending therapies accessible to billions, potentially removing the biological upper limit on human lifespan.

## Key Points

- The first human epigenetic reprogramming trial is days away, targeting blindness in the eye using three Yamanaka genes, with future plans to treat the liver, brain, and joints [00:02:11](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4IM866W7yGc&t=131).
- Sinclair's lab is utilizing AI to screen billions of molecules to create a cheap, small-molecule cocktail that can reverse cellular age, aiming for a cost comparable to Metformin rather than expensive gene therapies [00:06:55](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4IM866W7yGc&t=415).
- Personal longevity protocols include taking Resveratrol with fat, NMN, and Metformin or Berberine, while strictly avoiding daily alcohol and managing stress through meditation to reduce inflammation [00:24:44](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4IM866W7yGc&t=1484).
