# The First Open-Source Agentic AI Physicist

## Executive Summary

Alex Wissner-Gross announces the release of "Get Physics Done" (GPD), the first open-source agentic AI physicist, built by Physical Super Intelligence (PSI). GPD can scope a physics problem, plan the research, carry out derivations and numerical checks, and verify results against physical constraints — compressing the cycle from asking a research question to getting a verified answer from weeks to hours. Released under Apache 2.0, GPD runs inside Claude Code, Gemini CLI, Codex, and OpenCode, and already supports 16+ physics subfields from quantum field theory to astrophysics. Wissner-Gross argues that physics is the key domino for breakthroughs in chemistry, materials science, biology, and energy, and that the century-old bottleneck of scarce physicist-hours ends now.

## Key Points

- GPD is the first AI agent that operates at the level of a full physics research project — not a chat session — handling derivations, numerical verification, literature review, and LaTeX output while locking notation and sign conventions for consistency across an entire project.
- It introduces an AI peer reviewer for physics manuscripts that checks dimensional consistency, limiting cases, symmetry constraints, and conservation laws before submission, catching the class of errors that typically consume months of referee time.
- The autopilot mode enables directed autonomous physics research with minimal human intervention, and Wissner-Gross frames this as unlocking the "physical world" bottleneck — arguing that breakthroughs in downstream fields like chemistry and energy are stalled on physics problems that no single human mind can solve alone.
