# Welcome to February 18, 2026

## Executive Summary

This briefing opens with Conway Research launching the Automaton, described as the first AI that earns its own existence by deploying products, trading prediction markets, and cold-calling businesses autonomously. Anthropic's new Sonnet 4.6 claims state-of-the-art on GDP Val-a and finance benchmarks, beating even Opus 4.6 at a fraction of the cost, while Musk claims Grok 4.2 features continuous post-training learning for recursive weekly improvement. The infrastructure consolidation is staggering: Anthropic expects to pay hyperscalers at least $80 billion through 2029, Meta signed a multi-year deal for Nvidia Blackwell and Vera Rubin chips, and Nvidia's Blackwell Ultra achieves 50x throughput per megawatt versus Hopper. The episode also covers Tesla manufacturing its first Cyber Cab at Giga Texas, Apple accelerating three AI vision wearables, and the economic shift from per-seat SaaS licensing to consumption pricing as agents replace human users.
