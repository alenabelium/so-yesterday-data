# The First AI-Grown Land

## Executive Summary

Alex Wissner-Gross introduces Coastal Assembly PBC, a company using AI-optimized underwater structures to grow new land by programming the ocean to deposit sediment naturally. Field studies at Maldives resorts grew over 90 feet of new beach in six months and created an entirely new island. The thesis: just as the intelligence revolution collapsed the cost of compute, energy, and code, it is now reaching physical land — turning what was assumed to be permanently scarce into an abundant resource.

## Key Points

- AI-designed porous underwater structures dissipate storm energy while letting calm currents carry sediment inward, effectively making the ocean the construction crew — a field study at the JW Marriott Maldives grew 90+ feet of beach in 6 months [01:10](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aO9REOhyI0U&t=70)
- An intelligence platform reads satellite imagery, builds predictive shoreline models, and deploys autonomous surface vessels to place structures — each new site feeds ground truth back, creating a flywheel that improves with scale [02:39](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aO9REOhyI0U&t=159)
- Trillions of dollars in coastal property are priced on the assumption that land supply is fixed; island nations facing sea level rise could become land producers rather than land losers as AI-driven land creation matures [03:45](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aO9REOhyI0U&t=225)
