# 'Prompting' Just Split Into 4 Skills. You Only Know One. Here's Why You Need the Other 3 in 2026.

## Executive Summary

With autonomous agents now running for hours or days without human check-ins, the single skill most people call "prompting" has silently split into four distinct disciplines: prompt craft, context engineering, intent engineering, and specification engineering. The video argues that prompt craft (writing good chat-based instructions) is now table stakes, while the real leverage has shifted upward to curating optimal context windows, encoding organizational goals and guardrails, and treating an entire organization's document corpus as agent-readable specifications. Five foundational primitives -- self-contained problem statements, acceptance criteria, constraint architecture, decomposition, and evaluation design -- form the learnable building blocks of this new stack. Mastering these skills also improves human-to-human communication, as the clarity AI demands turns out to benefit teams and leadership as well.

## Key Points

- Prompting has diverged into four cumulative disciplines (prompt craft, context engineering, intent engineering, specification engineering), each operating at a different altitude and time horizon; skipping a layer creates failures that compound at scale.
- The five learnable primitives of specification engineering are self-contained problem statements, acceptance criteria, constraint architecture, task decomposition, and evaluation design -- and they transfer directly to clearer human communication and leadership.
- Small businesses and solo operators have the greatest advantage right now because they can quickly make their entire knowledge base agent-readable, while large organizations face enormous cultural and organizational inertia in adopting these new disciplines.
