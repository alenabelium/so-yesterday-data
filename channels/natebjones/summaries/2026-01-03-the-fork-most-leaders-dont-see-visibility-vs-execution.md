# The Fork Most Leaders Don’t See: Visibility vs. Execution

## Executive Summary

Nate argues that AI is creating a dangerous fork for leaders: the temptation to invest in AI-powered visibility (dashboards, metrics, single-pane-of-glass reporting) versus investing in AI-powered execution through small, empowered tiger teams. Drawing on the concept of legible vs. illegible work, he warns that AI makes fake legibility cheaper than ever, producing Potemkin villages of dashboards and metrics that look empirical but obscure where real value is created. The real production engine of a company is small trusted teams doing messy, fast, illegible work, and AI should be used to leverage those teams rather than to surveil them.

## Key Points

- AI makes fake legibility dangerously cheap: AI-generated dashboards, risk scores, and productivity metrics can look authoritative without reflecting real outcomes, giving leadership overconfidence in the wrong map
- A 5-person AI-augmented tiger team can now produce what 20-30 people produced in traditional structures, making small empowered teams the real production units of a company
- Leaders should use AI as a cheap historian that reconstructs meaning after the work, not as a bureaucrat that dictates it from above; measure teams by outcomes and impact, not adherence to AI-generated plans
