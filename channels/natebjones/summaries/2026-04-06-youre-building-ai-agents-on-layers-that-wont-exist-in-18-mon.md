# You're Building AI Agents on Layers That Won't Exist in 18 Months. (What this Means for You)

## Executive Summary

The video argues that the current AI agent infrastructure is undergoing a foundational shift comparable to the move to cloud computing, moving from human-first tools to agent-first primitives. It breaks down six critical layers of this new stack—compute, identity, memory, tools, billing, and orchestration—highlighting that many current solutions are transitional shims rather than permanent standards. The speaker warns that building on unstable layers creates compounding reliability risks and transitional lock-in, urging builders to develop deep 'stack literacy' to navigate this evolving landscape.

## Key Points

- Builders must distinguish between mature infrastructure like sandboxed compute and transitional shims like email-based identity to avoid future migration costs [00:03:10](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7HP1jFJ9W1c&t=190).
- Reliability compounds negatively as agents depend on multiple primitives, making the adoption of robust orchestration and standard failure patterns essential for enterprise-scale deployment [00:19:45](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7HP1jFJ9W1c&t=1185).
- Developing 'stack literacy' across all six layers is now a mandatory skill for leaders and engineers to prevent agent sprawl and ensure long-term business viability [00:21:40](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7HP1jFJ9W1c&t=1300).
