# 44% of Companies Cut Their Managers. Here's What They Actually Lost.

## Executive Summary

The video argues that while 44% of companies are cutting management layers to leverage AI, they are often discarding critical human functions like sensemaking and accountability alongside automatable information routing. By analyzing three distinct organizational models—Moonshot AI, Block, and Meta—the speaker demonstrates that successful adaptation requires unbundling management tasks rather than simply compressing them. Ultimately, the most resilient organizations will be those that use AI to handle logistics while preserving human roles for strategic interpretation and employee development.

## Key Points

- Organizations should immediately audit their management workflows to automate 'routing' (information logistics) with AI while explicitly preserving human oversight for 'sensemaking' and 'accountability' to avoid cultural strain [00:01:32](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zhXgkQ3nYeE&t=82).
- Leaders can mitigate the risks of flattening by adopting a 'Directly Responsible Individual' (DRI) model that assigns temporary ownership of specific problems to experts, separating strategic sensemaking from the permanent role of a manager [00:20:25](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zhXgkQ3nYeE&t=1225).
- Managers seeking job security must pivot their focus from administrative tasks to high-value coaching and feedback, visibly demonstrating their unique ability to provide human context and emotional support that AI cannot replicate [00:29:43](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zhXgkQ3nYeE&t=1783).
