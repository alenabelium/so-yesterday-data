# 45 People, $200M Revenue. The Question Nobody's Asking About AI and Your Team Size.

## Executive Summary

The explosion of meetings (tripled since 2020) is not a meetings problem but a team-size problem that AI has made dramatically worse by raising per-person output without reducing coordination overhead. The video uses evolutionary psychology (Dunbar's numbers), military research, and software engineering (Brooks's Law) to argue that the human brain caps deep coordination at about five people -- and AI raises the penalty for exceeding that limit by an order of magnitude because each person now generates millions in value. Two archetypes emerge: scouts (solo operators with full AI toolkits for exploration) and strike teams (five-person units optimized for correctness, not volume). The critical reframing is that AI is not a cost-reduction tool but a force multiplier: a 500-person company should not shrink to 50, but should recognize it now has the productive capacity of 3,000-5,000 people and pursue a mission five to ten times larger.

## Key Points

- AI raised per-person output by an order of magnitude, which raised the coordination cost of each additional team member by the same order of magnitude -- making the optimal team size of five (supported by evolutionary psychology, military research, and software engineering) more critical than ever. [03:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hnwM01CpzmA&t=210)
- The correct response to AI is not headcount reduction but ambition expansion: restructure into five-person "strike teams" pointed at missions five to ten times larger than what was previously possible, while using "scouts" (solo operators) for rapid exploration. [09:32](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hnwM01CpzmA&t=572)
- Volume is no longer scarce -- correctness is; a team of five optimizes for correctness through shared context and mutual verification, while larger teams optimize for volume and generate meeting overhead that destroys value at a rate proportional to how productive each member has become. [07:37](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hnwM01CpzmA&t=457)
