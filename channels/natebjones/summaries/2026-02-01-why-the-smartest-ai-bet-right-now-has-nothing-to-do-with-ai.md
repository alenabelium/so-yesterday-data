# Why the Smartest AI Bet Right Now Has Nothing to Do With AI (It's Not What You Think)

## Executive Summary

The Davos "abundance for all" narrative around AI misses the point: while AI creates unprecedented abundance of intelligence, the real value concentrates at the bottlenecks that constrain actual throughput. These bottlenecks span physical infrastructure (energy, land, semiconductors), trust (verification and authentication in a world of synthetic content), integration (bridging general AI capability with specific organizational context), and coordination (getting humans to align on sharing AI's gains). The same bottleneck principle applies fractally to individual careers, where the old constraints (information access, skill acquisition) are dissolving while new ones emerge around taste, problem-finding, institutional knowledge, and execution.

## Key Points

- AI's bottlenecks are migrating downstream: physical infrastructure operates on multi-year timelines while software sprints ahead, creating a structural gap where whoever navigates permitting, energy sourcing, and construction fastest captures disproportionate value
- The $4.5 trillion integration gap identified by Cognizant represents value locked behind the challenge of connecting general AI capability to specific organizational context -- tacit knowledge, embedded practices, and relationships that are not promptable
- For individuals, the new career bottlenecks are taste and judgment (knowing which of 100 AI-generated options is right), problem-finding over problem-solving, institutional context that takes slow accumulation, and the ability to metabolize change without freezing
