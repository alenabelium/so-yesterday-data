# 48 Days. That's How Long Before the Helium Runs Out for AI Chips.

## Executive Summary

The video argues that the missile-damaged Ras Laffan plant in Qatar has created a critical bottleneck for AI development by cutting off 33% of the global helium supply required for semiconductor fabrication. This disruption, combined with rising LNG energy costs in East Asia, threatens to delay chip production and significantly increase prices for memory and logic chips through at least mid-2027. Furthermore, the crisis may structurally advantage China's domestic chip industry by forcing a geopolitical restructuring of energy and helium supply chains away from US allies.

## Key Points

- Procurement teams should immediately purchase compute resources and inventory now to avoid severe price hikes and delivery delays caused by the evaporating helium supply chain constraints. [21:08](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sTkqCREdMXo&t=1268)
- Strategic planners must account for a multi-year increase in AI inference costs as energy prices in East Asia rise and helium shortages force fabs to operate at reduced capacity. [12:06](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sTkqCREdMXo&t=726)
- Organizations should anticipate a long-term geopolitical shift where China gains a competitive advantage in chip economics by securing domestic helium and Russian energy supplies while Western allies face persistent shortages. [14:53](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sTkqCREdMXo&t=893)
