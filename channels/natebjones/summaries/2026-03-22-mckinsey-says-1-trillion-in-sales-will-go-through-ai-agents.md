# McKinsey Says $1 Trillion In Sales Will Go Through AI Agents. Most Businesses Are Invisible.

## Executive Summary

The video argues that as AI agents capture $1 trillion in sales, businesses must fundamentally restructure their internal data stacks to be 'agent-readable and writable' rather than just adding superficial chat interfaces. The speaker warns that legacy systems designed to block bots are now blocking valuable revenue, as agents require clean, structured data to evaluate complex product attributes and execute transactions autonomously. Companies that fail to migrate tribal knowledge from marketing copy into durable data schemas risk becoming invisible to the next generation of commerce.

## Key Points

- Businesses must move beyond simple API wrappers or MCP servers and deeply refactor their internal data stacks to ensure all product attributes are structured and legible for autonomous agents [08:53](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BE4RclIGDmY&t=533).
- Companies need to extract 'tribal knowledge' currently hidden in marketing copy or human intuition and convert it into durable, queryable data attributes that agents can trust for complex decision-making [22:14](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BE4RclIGDmY&t=1334).
- Executives should immediately benchmark their own systems against competitors by testing if an AI agent can successfully discover, evaluate, and transact on their products to identify visibility gaps [26:07](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BE4RclIGDmY&t=1567).
