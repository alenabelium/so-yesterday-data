# Stripe, Visa, Mastercard, Microsoft, Meta. All Building The Same Thing.

## Executive Summary

The video argues that the internet economy is undergoing a fundamental shift where power moves from sellers to buyers through the rise of AI agents. Major companies like Stripe, Visa, and Microsoft are building infrastructure to support this agentic commerce, where intent is formed before a user ever visits a seller's website. This transition requires businesses to expose structured data and payment authority to agents rather than relying on traditional marketing funnels to capture human attention. Ultimately, success will depend on a company's ability to be programmatically usable and trusted by software acting on behalf of consumers.

## Key Points

- The core shift in the internet economy is moving from seller-controlled funnels that capture human intent to a buyer-driven model where AI agents arrive with pre-formed intent and context [00:00:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XGvDbeoSN3E&t=0).
- Payment authority is relocating from the seller's checkout flow to the buyer's agent, requiring new infrastructure like scoped tokens and one-time use cards to manage trust and fraud [00:13:52](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XGvDbeoSN3E&t=832).
- Branding is evolving from emotional persuasion on landing pages to becoming a reliable, data-driven entry in a buyer's preference ledger that agents can reference and trust [00:23:41](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XGvDbeoSN3E&t=1421)
