# $1,000 a Day in AI Costs. Three Engineers. No Writing Code. No Code Review. But More Output.

## Executive Summary

The fundamental unit of computing is shifting from the instruction to the token -- from deterministic human-written code to purchased intelligence. OpenAI is reportedly building $2,000-$20,000/month AI employee tiers, StrongDM targets $1,000/day in token spend with three engineers and no hand-written code, and per-token inference costs are falling 10-200x per year. This creates three distinct developer career tracks: orchestrators (who specify outcomes and manage intelligence budgets), systems builders (who build agent frameworks and evaluation pipelines), and domain translators (non-developers with deep industry expertise who can now build tools). The middle of the old software engineering distribution -- competent application coders without deep systems or domain expertise -- is most exposed.

## Key Points

- Intelligence is now a purchasable commodity with Jevons Paradox dynamics: as tokens get cheaper, consumption explodes rather than decreases, with average enterprise AI spend at $85K/month (up 36% YoY) and 45% of organizations planning to exceed $100K/month. [10:15](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-bQcWs1Z9a0&t=615)
- AI-native companies operate at 3-5x or more revenue per employee versus traditional SaaS ($3.5M at Cursor vs $600K industry average), and this ratio will widen as tooling matures, eventually forcing larger organizations to restructure around intelligence throughput rather than headcount. [19:09](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-bQcWs1Z9a0&t=1149)
- The market is splitting along generalized scale vs specialized precision: enterprises compete on token volume with horizontal platforms, while startups and solopreneurs win on specificity and domain knowledge in niches that were never economically viable before -- distribution and customer intimacy beat raw compute advantage. [26:42](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-bQcWs1Z9a0&t=1602)
