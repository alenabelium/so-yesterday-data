# The OpenClaw Saga: Zuckerberg Begged This Developer to Join Meta. He Said No. Here's Who Got Him.

## Executive Summary

Peter Steinberger, creator of OpenClaw (the fastest-growing open-source project in GitHub history at 200,000+ stars), joined OpenAI after being courted by both Sam Altman and Mark Zuckerberg. Steinberger chose OpenAI for mission alignment -- building "an agent his mother can use" -- and access to frontier compute, while OpenClaw itself moves to an independent foundation following a Chrome/Chromium model. The hire gives OpenAI three critical assets: developer trust from a builder with authentic open-source credibility, deep architectural knowledge of what happens when AI agents get real system access, and a community of 600 contributors and hundreds of thousands of users.

## Key Points

- Steinberger built OpenClaw using Codex, accumulated 6,600 commits in January alone running 4-10 agents simultaneously, and was the most prominent unpaid promoter of Codex on Lex Friedman's podcast -- making him a strategic asset for OpenAI's developer relations beyond any marketing spend.
- The hire fills OpenAI's gap in consumer-facing agent products: Codex handles coding, ChatGPT handles conversation, but neither provides a persistent always-on personal agent for email, calendars, messaging, and file management -- exactly what OpenClaw demonstrated was possible.
- Steinberger shipped OpenClaw's most comprehensive security overhaul (40+ patches) just days before announcing the move, and his battle-tested experience with real-world agent security failures (one-click RCE, malicious skills, exposed instances) is operationally valuable knowledge that no theoretical security research can replicate.
