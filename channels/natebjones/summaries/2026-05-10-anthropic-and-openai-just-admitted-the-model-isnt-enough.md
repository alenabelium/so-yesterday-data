# Anthropic And OpenAI Just Admitted The Model Isn't Enough.

## Executive Summary

The video argues that recent AI security incidents, such as the McKinsey 'Lily' breach, are not merely technical failures but symptoms of outdated enterprise procurement processes that ignore the unique complexities of agentic workflows. It highlights that traditional SaaS purchasing sequences fail because they treat AI agents as simple users rather than autonomous entities requiring distinct authentication, auditing, and permission models. Consequently, major vendors are shifting their focus from just providing large language models to offering infrastructure that supports secure, governed agent interactions. The core thesis is that organizations must integrate technical teams earlier in the decision-making process to prevent unbounded liability and ensure AI strategies are viable before implementation.

## Key Points

- The McKinsey 'Lily' incident exposed a systemic failure where 22 out of 200 endpoints lacked authentication, revealing that the root cause was a lack of technical oversight in the procurement phase rather than simple engineering negligence [00:02:40](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EpJ0CjTJSag&t=160).
- Traditional SaaS procurement sequences break down with AI agents because agents require complex, cross-system permission mapping and auditing that human users do not, making implementation a strategic rather than downstream concern [00:07:39](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EpJ0CjTJSag&t=459).
- Major vendors like Anthropic, OpenAI, and Salesforce are rapidly releasing new enterprise services focused on governed action, permission-aware data, and forward-deployed engineers to address the infrastructure gaps left by traditional model-centric approaches [00:10:29](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EpJ0CjTJSag&t=629).
- Decision-makers must ask specific questions about how platforms distinguish between human and agent identities, and how the system behaves under pressure, to ensure technical defaults do not lead to unbounded security liabilities [00:14:55](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EpJ0CjTJSag&t=895).
