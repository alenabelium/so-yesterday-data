# Your SaaS Bill Just Got a Second Meter. You're About to Pay It.

## Executive Summary

The video argues that the SaaS pricing model is shifting from per-seat human licenses to a 'second meter' that bills for autonomous agent actions and work units. Major vendors like Salesforce, Microsoft, and ServiceNow are introducing complex credit systems that charge for operational tasks rather than just token usage or user access. This transition creates significant financial risk for businesses that fail to negotiate clear, transparent pricing structures before their agents become mission-critical. The speaker urges developers and procurement teams to scrutinize contracts for 'rent-seeking' clauses and to define fair licensing terms that distinguish between reading, writing, and executing work.

## Key Points

- Vendors are introducing 'agentic work units' as a second billing layer alongside traditional seats, as seen with Salesforce's Agent Force and Microsoft's Copilot Studio credits [00:01:29](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=adNErrz2aA0&t=89).
- Contractual policies, such as SAP's API restrictions, are becoming the primary gatekeeper for agent access, forcing buyers to negotiate usage rights before technical implementation [00:06:43](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=adNErrz2aA0&t=403).
- Developers must optimize agent operations by distinguishing between read, write, and execute actions to control costs, as billing is increasingly tied to operational outcomes rather than just token volume [00:11:57](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=adNErrz2aA0&t=717).
- Procurement teams should negotiate upfront by asking specific questions about caps, exportable logs, and whether failed actions count toward the bill to avoid hidden costs during renewal [00:14:32](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=adNErrz2aA0&t=872).
