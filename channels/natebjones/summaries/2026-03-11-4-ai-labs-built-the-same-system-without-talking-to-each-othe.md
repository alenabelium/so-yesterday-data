# 4 AI Labs Built the Same System Without Talking to Each Other (And Nobody's Discussing Why)

## Executive Summary

Four organizations -- Anthropic, Google DeepMind, OpenAI, and Cursor -- independently converged on the same multi-agent coordination architecture (decompose, parallelize, verify, iterate) without coordinating, suggesting this pattern is not a design choice but a discovered solution to how intelligence works at scale. The video argues that AI's "jagged frontier" was never an inherent property of intelligence but an artifact of asking models to solve every problem in one shot without organizational structure. Cursor's coding harness -- designed to build web browsers -- solved an open research-grade mathematics problem by running for four days with zero human guidance, demonstrating that agent harnesses generalize far beyond their original domain. The practical implication is that AI capabilities are now smooth across most knowledge work: any task that can be decomposed into verifiable sub-problems is accessible to agentic workflows, making "sniff-checking" (the meta-skill of evaluating whether output is correct) the most valuable competency in every job family.

## Key Points

- Four AI labs independently built the same multi-agent architecture (decompose, parallelize, verify, iterate) -- a convergence that mirrors how human organizations have always solved complex problems, suggesting this is a fundamental pattern for scaling intelligence rather than an AI-specific innovation.
- AI's "jagged frontier" was an artifact of single-turn interaction without organizational structure; with proper agent harnesses, capabilities are now smooth across most knowledge work -- the relevant question shifts from "can AI do this specific task" to "can this work be decomposed into verifiable sub-problems."
- The meta-skill of "sniff-checking" (evaluating whether output is correct) becomes the most valuable competency in every job family as execution gets cheaper, and the organizations that figure out how to structure delegation to agents with clear verifiability will define the next era of work.
