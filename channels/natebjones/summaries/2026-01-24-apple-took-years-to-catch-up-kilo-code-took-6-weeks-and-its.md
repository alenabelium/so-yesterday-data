# Apple Took Years to Catch Up. Kilo Code Took 6 Weeks--and It's Coming for Lovable, Cursor, Replit

## Executive Summary

This weekly AI news roundup covers five stories. xAI closed a $20B Series E at $230B valuation despite active safety investigations in five countries, cementing a four-player race (OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI, Google) with clear runway to survive the scaling race. At Davos, Dario Amodei and Demis Hassabis agreed AGI is approaching but disagreed on timeline -- Amodei predicts 2026-2027, Hassabis gives 50% probability by decade's end and argues that automating 95% of a job only increases the value of the remaining 5% humans do. Apple admitted defeat on foundation models and signed a billion-dollar-per-year deal with Google to base its AI on custom Gemini models, a major blow to OpenAI's distribution ambitions. DeepSeek published Engram, a token-efficient conditional memory architecture. Kilo Code, founded by GitLab's co-founder, launched an engineer-focused app builder after a six-week sprint, targeting the gap between Lovable and Cursor.

## Key Points

- Three labs (OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI) plus Google now have clear multi-year funding runway for the scaling race; investor willingness to fund through active safety investigations signals a very long-term perspective on AI value. [01:18](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2sY7Pcm2j2g&t=78)
- Amodei and Hassabis disagree on AGI timelines, but Hassabis's view that automating most of a job increases the value of remaining human capabilities appears to match current employment data better than predictions of wholesale displacement. [02:40](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2sY7Pcm2j2g&t=160)
- Apple's billion-dollar Gemini deal shifts the default AI OS on both iOS and Android to Google, pressuring OpenAI and Sam Altman to find alternative distribution through a potential third device. [05:06](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2sY7Pcm2j2g&t=306)
