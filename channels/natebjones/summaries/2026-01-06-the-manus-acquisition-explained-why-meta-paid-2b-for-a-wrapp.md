# The Manus Acquisition Explained: Why Meta Paid $2B for a "Wrapper"

## Executive Summary

Meta acquired Manus for over $2 billion not for a model but for its agentic harness, the engineering that enables AI agents to reliably finish multi-step tasks. Nate explains that Manus pioneered techniques for long-running agents including KV cache optimization, goal re-articulation during extended tool-call loops, and restorable compression using the file system as persistent external memory. Meta likely intends to integrate these capabilities into automated ad building, aligning with their public goal of requiring only a wallet to advertise on Meta platforms. Nate also reviews three alternatives: Claude Code, GenSpark, and Do Anything.

## Key Points

- The acquisition signals that agent harnesses (the "car") are becoming more valuable than the underlying models (the "engine"); reliable task completion is a scarce commodity that justified the $2B price
- Manus differentiated by solving the finish-what-you-start problem through innovations in KV cache management, goal re-articulation over long task loops, and file-system-based persistent memory
- Integration into Meta will be extremely difficult (less than 10% chance of successful scaling in 2026); meanwhile, Claude Code, GenSpark, and Do Anything represent viable alternatives with different strengths
