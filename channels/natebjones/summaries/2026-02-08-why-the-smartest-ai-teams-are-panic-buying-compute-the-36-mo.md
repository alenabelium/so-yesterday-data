# Why the Smartest AI Teams Are Panic-Buying Compute: The 36-Month AI Infrastructure Crisis Is Here

## Executive Summary

A structural inference compute crisis is emerging that will not resolve before 2028. Enterprise AI consumption is growing at 10x annually, DRAM prices have surged 50%+ with another 55-60% increase projected for Q1 2026, semiconductor fab capacity is fully allocated, and hyperscalers have locked up GPU allocation for years in advance. Agentic systems compound the problem exponentially -- a single agent workflow can consume more tokens in an hour than a human generates in a month. Google processed 1.3 quadrillion tokens per month, a 130-fold increase in just over a year, serving as the leading indicator for enterprise demand curves.

## Key Points

- Hyperscalers are not neutral infrastructure providers but competitors: Google, Microsoft, and Amazon use their compute allocation to power their own AI products (Gemini, Copilot, Alexa) and will prioritize internal use over enterprise customers when supply is scarce -- every GPU sold to a customer is one unavailable for their own products
- A 10,000-person enterprise at current heavy usage ($20M/year) could face a $2B annual compute bill when agentic systems reach 100 billion tokens per worker; traditional IT planning frameworks (3-5 year depreciation, predictable demand) are broken in this environment
- The strategic playbook for CTOs: secure capacity contractually now before the crisis peaks, build an intelligent routing layer that abstracts infrastructure and preserves provider optionality, treat hardware as a 2-year consumable, and invest aggressively in efficiency (smaller models, caching, quantization) as a competitive advantage
