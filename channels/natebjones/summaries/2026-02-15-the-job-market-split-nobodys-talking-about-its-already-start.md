# The Job Market Split Nobody's Talking About (It's Already Started). Here's What to Do About It.

## Executive Summary

The knowledge workforce is bifurcating into two classes: those who drive high-value tokens by specifying precisely, architecting systems, and managing agent fleets (capturing extraordinary economic returns), and those operating at low leverage with AI-assisted but not AI-directed workflows (being commoditized). The bottleneck in software and knowledge work has shifted from production to specification -- the ability to translate vague human intent into precise, testable instructions that agents can execute. All knowledge work is converging on the same substrate as software engineering, requiring verifiable outputs, systems thinking, and engineering-level precision, because AI agents cannot fill in the gaps the way human colleagues do.

## Key Points

- Entry-level developer postings are down 67%, new graduates represent only 7% of hires (a historic low), and 70% of hiring managers say AI can do interns' work -- the junior pipeline is collapsing because low-leverage work is automated first.
- When production cost falls to zero, demand explodes (as with desktop publishing and mobile): the total addressable market for software is constrained by cost, not demand, meaning every business process currently running on spreadsheets, email, or phone calls becomes automatable.
- Knowledge workers must adopt engineering mindset practices: write specs with testable acceptance criteria, learn to work with compute (not just learn about it), make outputs verifiable, think in systems rather than documents, and honestly audit whether their role exists primarily because of organizational coordination overhead.
