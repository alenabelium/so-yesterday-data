# 90% of AI Users Are Getting Mediocre Output. Don't Be One of Them (Stop Prompting, Do THIS Instead)

## Executive Summary

Most AI users get mediocre output because models are trained through reinforcement learning from human feedback to produce statistically average responses that satisfy the broadest audience -- like Pizza Hut optimizing for the middle rather than delighting anyone specifically. Beyond prompting, there are four underused levers to escape this averaging: memory (retaining context across conversations), instructions (persistent behavioral guidance), apps and tools (MCP connections to external services), and style controls (tone and communication preferences). The people getting 10x results are those who systematically capture corrections and encode them back into these settings, creating a compounding personalization effect over time.

## Key Points

- AI models optimize for outputs that human raters (not you) would score highly, encoding "what would most people want" as the target -- your output always feels slightly off because it is calibrated to a hypothetical typical person, not your specific constraints and preferences [03:55](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KX0GurmgAoo&t=235)
- The four levers beyond prompting are memory (ChatGPT's saved memories and chat history, Claude's project-scoped memory, Gemini's Google app integration), instructions (custom instructions and CLAUDE.md files), tools (MCP connectors to external services), and style controls (personality presets and custom voice profiles) [12:40](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KX0GurmgAoo&t=760)
- Vague steering like "be concise" does nothing; specific instructions like "when I'm stuck, ask diagnostic questions rather than giving solutions" or "I've done product for 15 years, skip fundamentals and go straight to nuance" dramatically change output quality [14:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KX0GurmgAoo&t=886)
