# LeCun Said LLMs Are a Dead End—Then Revealed Meta Fudged Their Benchmarks. Both Matter - Here's Why.

## Executive Summary

This weekly AI news roundup covers five major stories. OpenAI and Anthropic both launched healthcare products, partly to serve real consumer demand and partly to build compelling IPO narratives in a regulated, high-spend vertical. Yann LeCun departed Meta, confirming that Meta fudged Llama 4 benchmarks and reiterating his belief that LLMs are a dead end for superintelligence. Physical AI hit a tipping point with Nvidia's Ruben platform, the Google DeepMind/Boston Dynamics partnership, and new open models for autonomous vehicles. A buried Wired report revealed OpenAI is paying contractors to upload real on-the-job work products, signaling that public training data is exhausted. Finally, the Claude Code explosion and ChatGPT 5.2 building a browser from scratch in one week demonstrate that AI coding capabilities have crossed a critical threshold, with Claude Co-work now extending that momentum into non-technical knowledge work.
