# Google's New AI Is Smarter Than Everyone's But It Costs HALF as Much. Here's Why They Don't Care.

## Executive Summary

Google shipped Gemini 3.1 Pro, leading on 13 of 16 benchmarks and scoring 77.1% on ARC-AGI2 (a 46-point jump in 90 days, the largest single-generation reasoning gain ever), at roughly 1/7th the cost of Opus 4.6. Yet Google does not need you to use it -- they generate $100B+ in annual free cash flow from search and advertising, design their own TPU silicon (which Anthropic and Meta pay to use), and view AI models as experiments in intelligence funded by an economic engine that has nothing to do with model subscriptions. Gemini 3.1 Pro is the strongest "naked reasoner" while Opus 4.6 is the strongest "equipped reasoner" (best at combining intelligence with tool use over sustained workflows), revealing that "hard" is not one thing.

## Key Points

- Gemini Deep Think collaborated with researchers to solve 18 previously unsolved problems across mathematics, physics, computer science, and economics -- including disproving a conjecture that had stood since 2015 and crossing disciplinary boundaries (geometric functional analysis to solve a graph theory problem) that human specialists rarely bridge.
- Most knowledge work difficulty is not reasoning but effort (auditing 3,000 contracts), coordination (aligning six teams), emotional intelligence (delivering feedback during a divorce), judgment/willpower (killing a project after six months), domain expertise (300 closed deals of pattern recognition), or ambiguity (deciding what to build when signals conflict) -- and each dimension is being automated on a very different timeline by different tools.
- Model routing is now a critical skill: matching problem types to the right model at the right price (Gemini Flash for classification, 3.1 Pro on max for novel reasoning, Opus 4.6 for sustained agentic work) creates real leverage, and the people who route well will dramatically outperform those using one model for everything.
