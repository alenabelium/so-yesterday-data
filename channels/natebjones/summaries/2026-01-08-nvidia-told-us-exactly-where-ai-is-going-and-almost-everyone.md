# NVIDIA told us exactly where AI is going — and almost everyone heard it wrong

## Executive Summary

Nate analyzes CES 2026 as the moment AI transitioned from a chip race to a factory race. Nvidia's Vera Rubin platform is not just a GPU but a full rack-scale AI factory optimized for inference economics, handling 10-million-token context windows at 10x lower cost. OpenAI has secured its competitive position through a portfolio of supply deals: 10 GW with Nvidia, 6 GW with AMD, 10 GW of custom silicon with Broadcom, plus cloud contracts with AWS and Coreweave. The underlying story is that AI demand massively outstrips compute supply, and the competition is now defined by delivered tokens per dollar, with memory, networking, and power as binding constraints alongside raw compute.

## Key Points

- Nvidia's Vera Rubin is a rack-scale platform that productizes inference context memory (KV cache management), signaling that inference scaling is now a memory and data movement problem as much as a compute problem
- OpenAI has built a multi-vendor supply portfolio (Nvidia, AMD, Broadcom, AWS, Coreweave, Samsung/SK Hynix) totaling tens of gigawatts, ensuring competitive positioning through 2027 regardless of market shifts
- Demand is so high that Nvidia will remain the dominant player while second-source ecosystems (AMD, custom silicon, Google TPUs) gain meaningful share, creating a multi-winner hardware landscape rather than a zero-sum competition
