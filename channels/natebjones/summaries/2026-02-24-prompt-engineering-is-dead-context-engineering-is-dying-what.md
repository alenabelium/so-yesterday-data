# Prompt Engineering Is Dead. Context Engineering Is Dying. What Comes Next Changes Everything.

## Executive Summary

Klarna's AI agent handles 2.3 million conversations monthly across 23 markets, does the work of 853 employees, and saved $60 million -- yet its CEO admitted the cost of the rollout exceeded the savings because the agent optimized for resolution speed rather than customer relationships. This illustrates the gap between context engineering (what AI needs to know) and "intent engineering" (what AI needs to want), the discipline of encoding organizational purpose -- goals, values, tradeoffs, decision boundaries -- as machine-readable, machine-actionable parameters. With 84% of companies not having redesigned jobs around AI and only 21% having mature agent governance, the intent gap explains why 74% of companies report no tangible value from AI despite massive investment.

## Key Points

- Intent engineering operates across three layers: unified context infrastructure (composable, vendor-agnostic systems connecting agents to organizational data via MCP), a coherent AI worker toolkit (shared workflows and capability maps rather than shadow agents), and intent alignment proper (machine-readable goal structures, delegation frameworks with decision boundaries, and feedback mechanisms). [12:48](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QWzLPn164w0&t=768)
- Microsoft Copilot illustrates the intent gap at scale: 85% of Fortune 500 adopted it, but only 5% moved from pilot to deployment and only 3% of Microsoft 365 users became paid users -- deploying AI across an organization without organizational intent alignment is like hiring 40,000 employees without telling them what the company does. [08:44](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QWzLPn164w0&t=524)
- Organizations must develop machine-readable expressions of intent that go far beyond "put OKRs in the prompt" -- agents need decomposed decision logic (when customer request X conflicts with policy Y, here is the resolution hierarchy), value trade-off hierarchies, and escalation boundaries, because unlike human employees, agents cannot absorb company culture through osmosis.
