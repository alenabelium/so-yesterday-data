# Why Your Product Gets Worse Every Time the Model Gets Better.

## Executive Summary

Nate Jones argues that AI app builders like Lovable and Replit are trapped in a "middleware" position where better models from Anthropic and OpenAI can instantly commoditize their products, since their differentiation is just a UI layer on top of someone else's intelligence. The video identifies five durable verticals of value that persist regardless of how good models get: trust (verification and payment layers), context (proprietary organizational data), distribution (curation and discovery), taste (human judgment and design sensibility), and liability (accountability and governance). Jones contends that in the emerging agentic economy, the companies that survive will own something structural the model providers cannot replicate, and the web will reorganize around these five pillars rather than around who builds software fastest.

## Key Points

- The AI app builder space is collapsing into a commodity: Lovable has 100,000 new projects created per day at $300M+ ARR, but most competitors are functionally thin wrappers around the same base models, and training your own model is not what separates survivors -- owning something structural like Replit's runtime or Vercel's deployment infrastructure is what matters. [00:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ib2m9HVX7as&t=0)
- Trust becomes a walled garden for the agentic web: when AI agents autonomously transact on your behalf, trust signals from companies like Stripe, Shopify, and Apple become the routing layer for responsible web traffic, and agents that cannot verify a service will simply refuse to use it. [07:07](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ib2m9HVX7as&t=427)
- When production becomes free, taste and liability emerge as critical human differentiators: taste is the conviction about what should exist that is not derivable from training data (like music producers thriving in the Suno era), while liability creates governance layers because "the AI did it" will not survive court, making accountability professionals essential to the agentic economy.
