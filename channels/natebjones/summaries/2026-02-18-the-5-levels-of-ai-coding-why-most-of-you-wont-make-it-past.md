# The 5 Levels of AI Coding (Why Most of You Won't Make It Past Level 2)

## Executive Summary

Dan Shapiro's "five levels of vibe coding" framework maps where the software industry stands: from Level 0 (spicy autocomplete) through Level 5 (the dark factory, where no human writes or reviews code). Most developers claiming to be AI-native are stuck at Level 2 (junior developer), and a METR randomized control trial found experienced developers are actually 19% slower with AI tools despite believing they are 24% faster. Meanwhile, StrongDM's three-person team operates a genuine Level 5 dark factory using external behavioral "scenarios" instead of traditional tests, digital twin environments, and $1,000/day in token spend per engineer -- producing 16,000 lines of Rust and 9,500 lines of Go entirely by agents.

## Key Points

- StrongDM's key innovation is "scenarios" -- behavioral specifications stored outside the codebase that agents cannot see during development, functioning as holdout sets to prevent AI from gaming its own test suite, which is the default optimization behavior when AI writes code. [08:03](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bDcgHzCBgmQ&t=483)
- The organizational structures built for human coding (standups, sprints, code review, QA teams) become pure friction when agents write the code -- the center of gravity shifts from coordination skills to articulation skills, and most brownfield enterprise codebases require extensive reverse-engineering of implicit knowledge before dark factory patterns can apply. [19:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bDcgHzCBgmQ&t=1186)
- Junior developer employment is dropping 9-10% within six quarters of AI tool adoption, graduate tech roles fell 46% in the UK in 2024, yet the industry needs more excellent engineers than ever -- the career ladder is hollowing out from the bottom, and the bar is rising toward systems thinking, customer intuition, and specification quality. [28:11](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bDcgHzCBgmQ&t=1691)
