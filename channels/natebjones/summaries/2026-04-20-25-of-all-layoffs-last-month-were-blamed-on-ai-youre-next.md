# 25% of All Layoffs Last Month Were Blamed on AI. You're Next.

## Executive Summary

Nate Jones argues that AI-driven code generation has broken the traditional chain by which professionals prove their value -- production used to be hard, hard signified effort, and effort signified expertise -- and this collapse is hitting everyone from junior engineers to mid-career PMs. With over 60,000 confirmed tech layoffs in Q1 2026 alone (Oracle 30K, Amazon 16K, Dell 11K), companies are reassessing how many people they need when AI handles generation. Jones proposes five principles to navigate this: prioritize comprehension over generation, ship structured explanations with your work, focus on transactions over credentials, work in the open, and make your proof of thinking inseparable from what you build. He introduces Talent Board as a platform for making this kind of "proof of thought" visible and shareable.

## Key Points

- The old value chain of "production is hard, therefore effort, therefore expertise" is broken because AI makes generation essentially free, meaning everyone from college grads to senior PMs now struggles to prove what they are actually worth. [03:40](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-dJ9WrTG6zQ&t=220)
- Comprehension over generation is the key principle: one project you fully understand teaches more than ten you vibe-coded without thinking, and the gap between what software does and what anyone thinks it does keeps widening, as illustrated by an AWS engineer whose AI tool deleted a production environment causing 13 hours of downtime. [05:41](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-dJ9WrTG6zQ&t=341)
- In the age of AI, professionals need "microtransactions for jobs" -- a richer, faster history of real paid work that replaces the traditional resume, combined with working in the open so proof of thinking travels with the work itself rather than staying locked behind company walls. [14:14](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-dJ9WrTG6zQ&t=854)
