# The new rules of AI product management 

## Executive Summary

Cat Woo, Head of Product at Anthropic, argues that the core of AI product management has shifted from long-term roadmapping to rapid iteration and defining clear goals for current model capabilities. She emphasizes that as code becomes cheaper, 'product taste'—the ability to decide what to build and how to guide users—is the most valuable skill, requiring PMs to remove barriers and ship features weekly. The episode highlights that successful AI teams prioritize a unified mission over individual product lines, allowing them to make fast, aligned decisions while accepting that product consistency may temporarily suffer for the sake of speed.

## Key Points

- PMs must shift from multi-quarter planning to weekly shipping cycles by setting clear, narrow goals and using 'research preview' launches to reduce friction and gather immediate user feedback [05:26](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PplmzlgE0kg&t=326).
- As engineering costs drop, the most critical skill for PMs is 'product taste'—the ability to prioritize the right problems and define the user experience rather than just managing the execution of code [18:16](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PplmzlgE0kg&t=1096).
- Teams should embrace a unified mission that supersedes individual product goals, enabling them to make rapid trade-offs and sacrifice specific features if they do not align with the broader organizational objective [29:32](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PplmzlgE0kg&t=1772).
- Building effective AI products requires creating rigorous evaluations (evals) to quantify success and understanding the specific limitations of current models to design harnesses that elicit maximum capability [53:32](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PplmzlgE0kg&t=3212)
