# Negotiation Expert: How to stop leaving 40% of your comp on the table 

## Executive Summary

Jacob Warwick, a professional behind-the-scenes negotiator who has helped clients secure over $1 billion in additional compensation, reveals the specific tactics and psychology of comp negotiation that product managers, engineers, and designers consistently underutilize. He explains that a simple pushback of "what's the chance there could be a little more" typically yields 20% improvement, while his structured approach averages 40% gains by treating the job search like an enterprise sales process. Jacob emphasizes that negotiation starts long before the offer letter, should never happen over email, and is fundamentally a collaboration rather than confrontation -- with the key being to understand the value you create for the company and make the hiring manager visualize a pain-free future with you on their team.

## Key Points

- Never negotiate over email because you cannot control how or when it is read; always push for video calls or in-person meetings where you can read body language, correct misunderstandings in real time, and control timing by scheduling conversations during your peak hours rather than the other party's convenience. [17:44](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pEis2CBomVA&t=1064)
- Treat the interview process like an enterprise B2B sales cycle: lead with discovery questions about their problems rather than talking about your past, build champions across the organization, and "sell the vacation" by walking the hiring manager through a future where you have solved their pain -- this creates leverage that makes salary bands negotiable. [40:35](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pEis2CBomVA&t=2435)
- When asked about comp expectations, deflect with "I don't discuss compensation until we are ready to make an offer" and redirect to understanding scope; if pressed, ask "what did you have in mind?" -- Jacob went from $14/hour to $120K/year with this single technique, and warns that anchoring to a number too early creates a ceiling that companies will split the difference on. [32:29](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pEis2CBomVA&t=1949)
