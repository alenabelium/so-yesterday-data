# How to be a CEO when AI breaks all the old playbooks 

## Executive Summary

Brian Halligan, co-founder and longtime CEO of HubSpot turned Sequoia in-house CEO coach, discusses what it takes to be a successful CEO in the AI era. He shares his "LOCKS" framework for evaluating CEOs (Lovable, Obsessed, Chip on shoulder, Knowledgeable, Student) and describes a new breed of "five-tool" CEOs who can code, design, sell, recruit, and set vision. While much of the conversation covers timeless leadership topics like hiring, giving feedback, and building executive teams, Halligan offers pointed observations about how AI is transforming go-to-market strategy and predicts that AI avatars will reshape sales funnels, websites, and buyer journeys within a few years.

## Key Points

- Halligan observes that software development and customer support have been dramatically transformed by AI at HubSpot, but go-to-market functions remain largely unchanged; he predicts the top of the funnel will shift to AI discovery platforms like Gemini and Claude, with company websites replaced by all-knowing AI avatars that handle the sales conversation. [28:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3UyitfSbY6c&t=1680)
- He identifies a new class of "five-tool CEOs" like Brett Taylor who can code, have taste, set vision, sell product, and recruit talent -- a combination he says was rare among previous generation CEOs like Jobs or Bezos, and notes that AI may enable non-technical founders to close the gap. [22:41](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3UyitfSbY6c&t=1361)
- Starting a company has never been easier but scaling has never been harder due to exploding competition; Halligan advises that the number of companies will mushroom in the next decade and distribution -- learning how to break through the noise -- is the critical differentiator that the fastest-learning founders will master. [27:09](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3UyitfSbY6c&t=1629)
