# How to show up in any room with a low heart rate: Silicon Valley’s missing etiquette playbook

## Executive Summary

Sam Lessin, partner at Slow Ventures and former VP of Product at Facebook, makes the case that proper etiquette is an underappreciated skill in Silicon Valley, framing it as "how to show up in a room with a low heart rate." In an era where technology is commoditized and trust matters more than ever, knowing how to introduce yourself, hold conversations, behave at meals, and navigate social situations gives founders and operators an unnecessary uphill battle if ignored. The conversation walks through practical rules across introductions, conversations, meetings, and meals, emphasizing an abundance mindset over a scarcity-driven, transactional approach to networking.

## Key Points

- Etiquette is reframed as a skill for showing up with a low heart rate: instead of arriving at a VC holiday party like a frantic Energizer Bunny trying to extract value from every interaction, approach with the calm of abundance -- this is part of the story, not the entire story, and you will have other opportunities. [07:18](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KtKJ3A6DWTs&t=438)
- Practical tips for introductions and conversations: be early (it lowers your heart rate), repeat people’s names back when you meet them, maintain eye contact, introduce your partner first, and use the "let it hang" trick when you forget someone’s name by introducing your companion and letting the other person fill in the gap. [10:10](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KtKJ3A6DWTs&t=610)
- Conversations should feel like ping-pong rather than an interrogation: asking questions is great but do not fire six in a row without giving anything back. Match your vocabulary to the person you’re speaking with, welcome others into the conversation rather than monopolizing a high-status person, and always approach with a give-before-you-take mindset. [16:21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KtKJ3A6DWTs&t=981)
