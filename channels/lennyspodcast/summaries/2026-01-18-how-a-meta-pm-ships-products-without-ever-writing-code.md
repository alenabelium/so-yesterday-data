# How a Meta PM ships products without ever writing code 

## Executive Summary

Zevy Arnowitz, a product manager at Meta with zero technical background, demonstrates his full workflow for building products entirely with AI tools. Starting from a ChatGPT project he used as a "virtual CTO," he graduated through Bolt and Lovable to Cursor with Claude Code, developing a structured series of slash commands that take him from issue creation in Linear through exploration, planning, execution, code review, and documentation updates. He live-builds a feature for his revenue-generating side project Studymate, and showcases a clever peer-review technique where he has Claude, Codex, and Gemini review each other's code to catch bugs he cannot identify himself. The conversation makes a compelling case that AI is collapsing the boundaries between technical and non-technical roles.

## Key Points

- Zevy's full workflow uses a chain of slash commands in Cursor: /create-issue captures ideas into Linear, /exploration has Claude deeply analyze the codebase and ask clarifying questions, /create-plan produces a markdown plan with status tracking, /execute builds the feature, /review has Claude review its own code, and /peer-review pits different models against each other to catch bugs -- all prompts are shared for listeners to download and use directly. [14:22](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1em64iUFt3U&t=862)
- The peer-review technique is particularly powerful for non-technical builders: Claude acts as the communicative dev lead, Codex (GPT) is the silent genius who fixes the hardest bugs, and Gemini is the "crazy scientist" who produces terrifying intermediate steps but delivers beautiful UI. By having each model review the others' code and then giving the results back to the lead model to accept or reject, quality stays high without the builder needing to understand the code. [40:26](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1em64iUFt3U&t=2426)
- Zevy argues that using AI for product work does not atrophy PM skills but instead gives junior PMs reps at a much higher level -- strategy, marketing, full product decisions -- that they would never get in their day job. He also used AI extensively to prepare for his Meta PM interview, creating a coaching project in Claude, mock-interviewing with voice mode, and even building a quiz game in Base44 to practice market segmentation questions. [54:25](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1em64iUFt3U&t=3265)
