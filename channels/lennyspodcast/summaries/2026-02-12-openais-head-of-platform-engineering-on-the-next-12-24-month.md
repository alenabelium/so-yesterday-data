# OpenAI's head of platform engineering on the next 12-24 months of AI 

## Executive Summary

Sherwin Woo, head of engineering for OpenAI's API and developer platform, reveals that 95% of OpenAI engineers use Codex daily, 100% of PRs are reviewed by Codex, and engineers using Codex open 70% more PRs with the gap widening. He describes engineers as modern-day wizards managing fleets of 10-20 parallel agent threads, drawing on the SICP "sorcery" metaphor from 1980. Sherwin predicts a boom of micro-startups and a potential "golden age of B2B SaaS" as a second-order effect of the one-person billion-dollar startup thesis, and advises builders to design for where models are heading in 6-12 months rather than their current capabilities.

## Key Points

- OpenAI has a team maintaining a 100% Codex-written codebase with no escape hatch to manual coding; their key finding is that most agent failures stem from insufficient context rather than model limitations, leading them to encode more tribal knowledge into documentation and code structure. [12:12](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B26CwKm5C1k&t=732)
- Sherwin argues that most enterprise AI deployments have negative ROI because adoption is mandated top-down without bottom-up evangelism; his advice is to find and empower the technical-adjacent employees who are most excited about AI and let them build best practices, run hackathons, and create internal excitement. [39:21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B26CwKm5C1k&t=2361)
- Over the next 12-18 months, Sherwin expects models to handle multi-hour coherent tasks reliably and sees audio/speech as a hugely underrated domain for enterprise AI, noting that business process automation outside of software engineering represents a massive underappreciated opportunity. [50:16](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B26CwKm5C1k&t=3016)
