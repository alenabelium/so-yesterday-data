# The surprising advice from a founder who built 2 unicorns 

## Executive Summary

Jason Cohen, four-time founder (including two unicorns, most notably WP Engine), presents his diagnostic framework for what to do when product growth stalls. He walks through a prioritized series of questions: first check if customers are leaving (churn), then examine pricing and positioning, then assess channel saturation, and finally ask whether you actually need to grow at all. The conversation is grounded in Cohen's 20 years of blogging at a]smartbear.com and his experience investing in ~60 startups, making it an extremely practical and actionable playbook for founders and product teams experiencing decelerating growth.

## Key Points

- The first and most important diagnostic for stalled growth is churn: cancellations grow automatically as your customer base grows (they are a percentage), while marketing growth is linear and hard to scale. The maximum size your company can ever reach is simply new customers per month divided by your monthly churn rate -- at 5% churn and 100 new customers/month, you will never exceed 2,000 customers. [11:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8xLquwfx6p0&t=690)
- Pricing is almost always too low because founders guessed at launch and never revisited: a $2/month product signals to a 1,000-employee company that it cannot possibly be good enough. Cohen finds that raising prices often does not decrease signups, which means companies are leaving enormous revenue on the table simply by not testing higher price points. [00:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8xLquwfx6p0&t=0)
- The final and most provocative question is whether you actually need to grow at all, challenging the "if you're not growing, you're dying" mantra. Cohen suggests this may be investor-driven pressure that pushes founders to chase growth when they would be better served by optimizing what they already have. [00:58](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8xLquwfx6p0&t=58)
