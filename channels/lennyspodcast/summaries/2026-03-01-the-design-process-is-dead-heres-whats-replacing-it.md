# The design process is dead. Here's what's replacing it. 

## Executive Summary

Jenny Wen, head of design for Claude Co-work at Anthropic (formerly director of design at Figma), argues that the traditional design process of research-diverge-converge is effectively dead, killed not by designers choosing to change but by the rapid pace of engineering enabled by AI coding tools. She describes how design work has stratified into two modes: supporting fast execution alongside engineers running multiple AI agents, and creating short-horizon (3-6 month) product visions rather than multi-year design decks. Jenny shares that her time allocation has shifted from 60-70% mocking/prototyping to 30-40%, with the remainder split between pairing directly with engineers and writing code herself.

## Key Points

- The traditional design process is dead because engineers using tools like Claude Code can spin up seven parallel agents and ship features faster than designers can mock them; designers must shift from gatekeeping to consulting, helping guide execution quality and create cohesive experiences as they happen in real-time. [05:50](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eh8bcBIAAFo&t=350)
- Jenny still uses Figma for exploring 8-10 design directions simultaneously and fine visual/interaction details because coding tools are too linear for divergent exploration; she believes AI will get better at taste and judgment but humans will remain necessary for accountability and deciding what actually gets built. [20:39](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eh8bcBIAAFo&t=1239)
- Anthropic builds trust with users through speed rather than perfection, shipping research previews early with rough edges and then iterating rapidly on feedback; Jenny switched from management back to IC work specifically to stay close to how the design process is changing, gaining hard skills she believes are essential for empathetic leadership in this era.
