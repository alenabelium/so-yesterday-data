# We replaced our sales team with 20 AI agents—here’s what happened next 

## Executive Summary

Jason Lemkin, founder of SaaStr, shares how he replaced his 10-person go-to-market team with 1.2 humans and 20 AI agents after two salespeople quit at his annual event, achieving roughly the same business performance. He walks through the specific agents he deployed (Artisan for outbound, Qualified for inbound, Agent Force for reactivation, Deli for support) and emphasizes that these tools require 30+ days of hands-on training to work well -- they do not work out of the box. The conversation offers a candid look at how AI is displacing mid-pack salespeople while making top performers more valuable, and argues that anyone who personally trains and deploys an AI sales agent becomes highly employable in the current market.

## Key Points

- SaaStr went from ~10 go-to-market people to 1.2 humans plus 20 AI agents handling outbound, inbound qualification, and lapsed customer reactivation, with net productivity roughly equal to the previous human team. The key lesson: pick a leading vendor whose forward-deploy engineer will help you train the agent, not the one with the best feature matrix. [07:56](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I-R1bc1rlFs&t=476)
- AI agents trained on your best salesperson’s email copy and lightly personalized with CRM data produce emails that are "pretty good" -- not as good as a top rep’s hand-crafted outreach, but significantly better than the mediocre reps most companies actually have. After sending hundreds of thousands of emails, SaaStr found that disclosing the AI sender versus hiding it made no difference in response rates. [42:55](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I-R1bc1rlFs&t=2575)
- The classic email-based SDR and human lead-qualifier roles will be "mostly extinct" by next year, while managing and orchestrating AI agents is emerging as a new, highly valuable career path -- Lemkin advises anyone worried about their sales job to personally pick one agentic tool, train it themselves over 50-60 hours, and get it into production. [21:15](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I-R1bc1rlFs&t=1275)
