# A behind-the-scenes interview with Lenny Rachitsky on building his newsletter and podcast

## Executive Summary

In this special episode, Lenny Rachitsky's wife Michelle Rial turns the tables and interviews him about his journey building a 1.2 million subscriber newsletter and top-10 tech podcast. Lenny shares the personal moments that led him to this career -- from a psychedelic experience at Joshua Tree that gave him confidence to write, to the Lindy effect realization at 9 months of weekly publishing. The conversation is deeply personal, covering his origins from Odessa, Ukraine, his misophonia, his approach to stress management, and the constant "Indiana Jones boulder" feeling of producing weekly content, while Michelle shares insights into her own creative process making viral charts.

## Key Points

- Lenny describes how his newsletter started as plan D after leaving Airbnb, with no intention of becoming a media business; a friend's advice to follow the rare Venn diagram of enjoying something, being good at it, and people valuing it became the turning point that led him to add a paywall 9 months in. [06:47](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HEqrvF7ztBE&t=407)
- He took a University of Pennsylvania happiness course that taught him the science of raising one's baseline happiness level through optimistic thinking; combined with the Lindy effect concept (something that has lasted X time will likely last X more), these frameworks help him sustain a demanding weekly publishing cadence. [15:54](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HEqrvF7ztBE&t=954)
- Michelle explains that her viral charts work best when they are extremely simple, make her laugh or feel something even though she created them, and are easy to digest with short attention spans; Lenny notes the parallel to his newsletter where intrinsic quality judgment matters more than external validation. [22:09](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HEqrvF7ztBE&t=1329)
