# Dario Amodei (Anthropic) Drops ATOMIC BOMBSHELL at Davos!

## Executive Summary

Shapiro reacts to Dario Amodei's Davos interview claiming AI could achieve fully automated recursive self-improvement (RSI) within 6-12 months, with AI now writing nearly 100% of the code for some Anthropic researchers. He contextualizes this alongside DeepMind hiring a chief AGI economist and Elon Musk targeting smarter-than-human workers by end of 2026. Shapiro argues that we are entering an era of "cognitive hyperabundance" where intelligence per token is rising on a power law, and that all the operational ingredients for RSI -- mathematical ability, automated hypothesis testing, code generation, and validation pipelines -- are largely already in place. He tempers the hype by noting that RSI will still involve gated human oversight and that the next bottlenecks will shift to energy, chips, and data rather than raw intelligence.

## Key Points

- Dario Amodei's prediction of RSI within 6-12 months is credible given his track record -- he predicted AI would handle 75-90% of coding by end of 2025 and was directionally correct, off by only about 3 months, which suggests his timeline estimates deserve serious consideration. [13:40](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WNU078Hwgqs&t=820)
- We are entering "cognitive hyperabundance" where intelligence per token follows a power law -- small gains in model capability produce outsized improvements in output quality, similar to how humans need an IQ above roughly 135 to contribute to frontier physics, suggesting threshold effects matter more than linear scaling. [09:09](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WNU078Hwgqs&t=549)
- Even with fully autonomous RSI pipelines operational, the Skynet scenario is unrealistic because training runs require real money (triggering human oversight), safety evaluations involve separate teams and third parties, and the infrastructure itself is physically distributed and non-monolithic. [22:38](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WNU078Hwgqs&t=1358)
