# What is going on with AI?

## Executive Summary

David Shapiro addresses two sources of confusion in the AI discourse: the data center buildout and the misleading narratives from academics like Cal Newport. He argues that the AI data center buildout is the second-largest mega project in history (adjusted for GDP) and, unlike tulip manias, produces durable capital assets that retain value for decades. On the narrative side, he critiques academics who conclude AI is a "nothing burger" based on studies that use outdated models (GPT-3.5, Qwen 2) and flawed methodologies, while ignoring the lived experience of power users who are 10x to 100x more productive. His core thesis is that academia's structural two-to-three-year publishing lag makes it fundamentally incapable of keeping pace with AI's rate of change, so anyone relying solely on academic papers for their AI worldview is operating with dangerously stale information.

## Key Points

- The AI data center buildout is the second-largest mega project in history by GDP share (behind only the Marshall Plan) and the only one that is entirely privately funded, producing durable capital assets that last 50+ years rather than speculative bubbles like tulip mania. [01:53](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8ad3L_TkDqk&t=113)
- Academic critics like Cal Newport and Daron Acemoglu consistently use outdated AI models in their studies and take two to three years to publish, meaning their conclusions about AI's limitations are already obsolete by the time they reach the public. [13:43](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8ad3L_TkDqk&t=823)
- Real-world power users are achieving 10x or greater productivity gains with AI, but academia structurally ignores these anecdotes, and professors who are themselves highly productive with AI cannot openly demonstrate it because it would go over the heads of their peers. [20:50](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8ad3L_TkDqk&t=1250)
