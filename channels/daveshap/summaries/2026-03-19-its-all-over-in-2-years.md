# It's all over in 2 years

## Executive Summary

In a deeply personal essay-style video, David Shapiro reflects on the visceral realization that his intellectual contributions will be rendered obsolete within one to two years as AI follows a super-exponential growth curve. He describes the emotional shift from "vesperance" — wistful nostalgia for the ending era — to something darker, as the convergence of the Fourth Turning, long-term debt cycles, and the fourth industrial revolution reshapes every societal norm simultaneously. While acknowledging that creative and social dimensions of humanity cannot be automated (the number of possible novels alone exceeds atoms in the universe), he frames his work on post-labor economics as potentially his final rigorous contribution. The video captures the tension between intellectual optimism about the long-term future and the existential dread of living through the transition.

## Key Points

- AI already handles most of the heavy lifting for Shapiro's intellectual work, and he predicts that within one to two years, the value of human problem-solving drops to zero as machines become better, faster, cheaper, and safer across all cognitive domains. [00:00:01](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l_B3dg97dSM&t=1)
- Creative work remains mathematically inexhaustible — the number of viable novels in English alone ranges from 10^100 to 10^150,000, far exceeding the 10^80 atoms in the universe — meaning art, social commentary, and embodied experience will persist as human domains. [04:21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l_B3dg97dSM&t=261)
- Three major historical cycles — the Fourth Turning, Ray Dalio's long-term debt cycle, and the fourth industrial revolution — are converging simultaneously, making this disruption potentially faster and harder than any previous societal transformation. [01:28](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l_B3dg97dSM&t=88)
