# Energy! Chips! ...and INSURANCE? (WTF)

## Executive Summary

Shapiro conducts a deep dive into the real bottlenecks slowing AI acceleration as of early 2026, finding that the barriers are overwhelmingly physical rather than philosophical. The hardest constraint is energy -- the average US grid interconnection wait is 7 years, and data center demand is projected to hit 134 gigawatts by 2030. High-bandwidth memory has replaced GPUs as the supply chain bottleneck, with Nvidia booking over 50% of all chip-on-wafer-on-substrate capacity. The most surprising finding is that insurance companies have absolute AI exclusions in their policies because they cannot price AI risk, creating a significant barrier to enterprise adoption that outweighs public debates about safety and ethics.

## Key Points

- Energy is the hardest bottleneck with a 7-year average grid interconnection wait, data center demand projected to grow from 4 GW to 134 GW by 2030, and large grid transformers having a 210-week lead time -- nuclear solutions won't arrive until the 2030s, making natural gas turbines, solar, and iron-air grid-scale batteries the critical bridge technologies. [03:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UcXtm9iOX5E&t=211)
- GPUs are no longer the supply chain bottleneck -- high-bandwidth memory is sold out through end of year as manufacturers abandon legacy DDR3/DDR4 production for more profitable accelerator memory, and Nvidia books over 50% of all chip-on-wafer-on-substrate packaging capacity, creating downstream shortages for robots, autos, and laptops. [09:42](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UcXtm9iOX5E&t=582)
- The most ironic barrier to AI adoption is insurance: many policies have absolute AI exclusions, meaning if AI touches an incident involving an OSHA violation or patient death, insurers wash their hands entirely -- this creates intolerable financial and legal risk that outweighs all public debates about AI safety, ethics, and regulation combined.
