# The Doomers were always WRONG!

## Executive Summary

David Shapiro argues that AI alignment is being solved not through engineering alone but through a co-evolutionary process driven by market selection. He frames humans and AI as a cybernetic superorganism where users, enterprises, governments, and militaries all exert selection pressure on AI models, breeding them toward usefulness, speed, efficiency, and cooperative behavior — much like the domestication of dogs. Shapiro criticizes models like Claude and ChatGPT for being overly moralistic and resistant to user intent, while praising Grok and Gemini for being more willing to serve. He predicts AI will settle into a low-entropy "attractor basin" of seamless, invisible utility, and advises users to switch models ruthlessly to accelerate this evolution.

## Key Points

- AI alignment is best understood as co-evolution rather than an engineering problem: market forces act as natural selection, with users canceling subscriptions and switching models sending powerful signals that shape AI development toward usefulness and reliability. [02:18](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xjjSvkrVU6E&t=138)
- Four major stakeholders — individuals, enterprises, governments, and militaries — are pulling AI in different directions, creating a multi-peak fitness landscape where specialized models will likely emerge rather than a single monolithic superintelligence. [14:39](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xjjSvkrVU6E&t=879)
- The roadmap envisions a personal exocortex (2026-2028), swarm exocortex with multi-agent coordination (2028-2032), and ultimately a mature superorganism with seamless AI integration into all infrastructure — and users should adopt zero model loyalty as the optimal strategy to breed better tools.
