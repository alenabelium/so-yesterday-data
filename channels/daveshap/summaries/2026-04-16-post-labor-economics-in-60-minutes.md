# Post-Labor Economics in 60 minutes

## Executive Summary

The video defines post-labor economics as a future regime where human labor is no longer a binding constraint on output due to AI and robotics acting as a general-purpose technology. The speaker argues that while automation creates a 'deflationary death spiral' by decoupling wages from GDP, the solution lies in shifting household income sources from wages to government transfers and private capital ownership. Ultimately, the framework proposes a capitalist-friendly transition where citizens become investors in sovereign wealth funds and employee-owned trusts to maintain economic circulation without traditional employment.

## Key Points

- Recognize that AI functions as a general-purpose technology that unbundles jobs into tasks, making human labor economically irrational when machines are better, faster, cheaper, and safer [00:06:13](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AhAXZ4Cw9Nc&t=373).
- Prepare for the 'deflationary death spiral' by understanding that as automation reduces the need for wages, household income must be replaced by government transfers and capital returns to sustain aggregate demand [00:21:51](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AhAXZ4Cw9Nc&t=1311).
- Adopt a strategy of capital accumulation through mechanisms like sovereign wealth funds, employee stock ownership plans (ESOPs), and universal basic income to ensure economic viability in a post-labor world [00:35:06](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AhAXZ4Cw9Nc&t=2106).
