# I think the Singularity could be BORING

## Executive Summary

David Shapiro argues that the singularity will feel boring because our brains update their self-model so quickly that each transformative change becomes "normal" within weeks, and the biggest physical changes (energy, robotics, space infrastructure) will happen invisibly behind the scenes. He contends that in hindsight, it will be obvious that we invented computers to run AI and that all energy production is ultimately to power AI workloads. The most dramatic near-term change will be personalized medicine through home genomic analysis, but even that will feel mundane once normalized. Shapiro also raises a provocative counter-thesis: physics may be running out of surprises, the golden age of fundamental discoveries may be behind us, and progress may follow a sigmoid curve where intelligence alone cannot overcome physical limits on energy, distance, and time.

## Key Points

- Our brains evolved to rapidly update their self-model after changes — just as a healed caveman quickly forgets the misery of a broken arm to resume hunting, we normalize each AI breakthrough within weeks, which is why Claude Code operating autonomously for a week already feels like old news rather than a singularity-level event. [15:59](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iLCom31oY7c&t=959)
- Most of the singularity's physical manifestation will be invisible: robots will work in mines, warehouses, and factories behind the scenes; space infrastructure will be built at Lagrange points invisible to the naked eye; and in 20 years, 99%+ of all computation cycles will run AI workloads while human residential energy use may be less than 1% of grid demand. [08:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iLCom31oY7c&t=480)
- Physics may be running out of fundamental surprises — it has been a long time since we discovered a new particle or a fundamentally new kind of energy, the golden age of science fiction was written during the fastest rate of physics discoveries, and we may need to accept that intelligence alone cannot overcome physical limits like faster-than-light travel or the thermodynamic ceiling of Earth's surface.
