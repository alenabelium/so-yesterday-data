# We're already too late

## Executive Summary

David Shapiro presents a detailed blueprint for achieving "universal high income" — a target of $300,000 median household income by 2060 — built on stacking multiple non-wage income streams from capital and transfers. He demonstrates through a simulator that all household income comes from three buckets (wages, capital, transfers), and as wages decline due to automation, a portfolio of sovereign wealth funds, state permanent funds, municipal endowments, ESOPs, baby bonds, and UBI programs must replace them. Drawing on 140+ real-world interventions from Alaska to Norway to Singapore, he argues the math works in the long run but America is decades behind — sovereign wealth funds take 25-30 years to mature, while the automation cliff arrives in the 2030s, creating a dangerous gap that only transfer spending can bridge.

## Key Points

- All household income comes from just three buckets — wages (82%), transfers (13%), and capital (5%) — and as automation eliminates wage labor, the goal is to flip the ratio so capital provides 60%+ of household income through sovereign wealth funds, ESOPs, baby bonds, and auto-enrolled investment accounts. [04:14](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8jV3HcmiWV8&t=254)
- America is the only major advanced economy without a national sovereign wealth fund, and a fund capitalized at 3-5% of GDP growing at 7% returns takes 25-30 years to reach $58 trillion — meaning the returns will not be ready when the automation cliff hits around 2030-2035, requiring massive transfer spending as a stopgap. [20:58](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8jV3HcmiWV8&t=1258)
- The U.S. already transfers $4 trillion per year in entitlement spending and demonstrated the capacity to distribute funds at scale with COVID stimulus checks ($867 billion reaching 160 million households), proving the infrastructure exists to bridge the gap while capital-based income matures. [14:43](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8jV3HcmiWV8&t=883)
