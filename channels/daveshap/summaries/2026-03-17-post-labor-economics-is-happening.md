# Post-Labor Economics is happening.

## Executive Summary

David Shapiro announces the completion of his 180,000-word book on post-labor economics and recounts the 18-month journey of co-creation with his audience. He explains that the core thesis is that human labor input is decoupling from economic output, meaning wages will cease to be the primary source of household income and must be replaced by a combination of government transfers (UBI, social security) and capital-based income (stocks, businesses, rental properties). The book traces how the primary, secondary, and tertiary economic sectors have each peaked and declined historically, arguing the service sector peaked around 2015 and the same pattern will repeat. Shapiro frames the Kickstarter launch as the critical make-or-break moment to get the ideas in front of voters, politicians, and think tanks.

## Key Points

- Post-labor economics rests on the insight that all household income comes from just three buckets — wages, transfers, and capital — and as AI decouples economic output from human labor, the focus must shift to boosting both transfers and capital-based income to prevent a deflationary death spiral. [09:17](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2wVlcG8mfi4&t=557)
- The historical pattern of sector decline — primary (farming/mining) fell from 90% to under 2%, secondary (manufacturing) hollowed out industrial cities — is now repeating in the tertiary (services) sector, which appears to have peaked around 2015. [07:16](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2wVlcG8mfi4&t=436)
- The book was deliberately chosen over a blog series because a single cohesive work is the best vehicle to get university professors, politicians, and skeptical voters on the same page — Shapiro's idealized reader is his "conservative father-in-law." [04:51](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2wVlcG8mfi4&t=291)
