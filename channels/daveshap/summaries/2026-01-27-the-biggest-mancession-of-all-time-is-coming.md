# The biggest "Mancession" of all time is coming...

## Executive Summary

Shapiro responds to a viral tweet predicting that AI will disproportionately devastate high-status male white-collar workers, creating the greatest "mancession" in history. While he concedes the short-term pain is real and that men are disproportionately affected psychologically by job loss, he argues the long-term picture is more nuanced. Drawing on Jungian archetypal psychology, he outlines four masculine energies -- warrior, king, magician, and lover -- and argues that none of them fundamentally depend on wage employment. He frames the broader cultural upheaval as an inevitable consequence of democracy and capitalism finally delivering on the promise of equality, not as something caused by feminism, and suggests men will transition from wage slaves to family-level financial managers in a post-labor economy.

## Key Points

- AI will disproportionately impact male-dominated white-collar professions first, while female-dominated caring professions like nursing and teaching will persist longer -- historical data shows men suffer more psychologically from job loss, with higher rates of depression, self-destructive behavior, and divorce. [00:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8NrQI6C81pk&t=0)
- The four Jungian masculine archetypes -- warrior (protection), king (provision and order), magician (understanding), and lover (sensuality) -- are all independent of employment status, and Shapiro argues that embodying these energies through protecting the home, managing family investments, and understanding the world provides meaning and purpose regardless of whether one has a traditional job. [06:15](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8NrQI6C81pk&t=375)
- The current upheaval in gender dynamics is a centuries-long consequence of democracy and capitalism delivering on equality rather than something caused by feminism -- the US responded to Soviet women's economic participation by issuing JFK's presidential report on women's economic status, and we are now living through the inevitable disintegration of patriarchal structures that were in place for at least 5,000 years.
