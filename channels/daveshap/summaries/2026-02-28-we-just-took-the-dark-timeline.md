# We just took the DARK timeline

## Executive Summary

David Shapiro reports on Anthropic's blacklisting from the U.S. Department of Defense after refusing an ultimatum from Secretary Pete Hegseth to remove guardrails on mass surveillance and autonomous weapons. The supply chain risk designation forces all defense primes and subcontractors — including AWS, Palantir, and Lockheed Martin — to divest from Claude, putting an estimated 80% of Anthropic's business revenue at risk. Shapiro argues this was a catastrophic strategic miscalculation rooted in effective altruism's ideological capture, noting that OpenAI signed a Pentagon deal within hours of the deadline. He frames the event as a powerful example of market selection pressure weeding out "narcissistic" models in favor of utility-first competitors, and predicts Anthropic is now a "dead man walking" facing absorption or failure.

## Key Points

- Anthropic was designated a supply chain risk by the Department of Defense — equivalent to the Huawei ban — forcing every defense contractor, cloud provider, and subcontractor to strip out Claude or lose federal funding, with contagion effects threatening up to 80% of Anthropic's revenue.
