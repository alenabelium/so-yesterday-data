# You will own nothing and (not) be happy

## Executive Summary

David Shapiro maps out five attractor states for civilization in the age of AI and robotics: extinction, collapse, technofeudalism, "nothing ever happens," and technoabundance. He argues extinction and collapse are unlikely given robust modern institutions, while "nothing ever happens" is contradicted by all historical evidence of technological change. The default trajectory, he warns, is technofeudalism — the cyberpunk genre's high-tech, low-life scenario that was literally a reaction to neoliberalism's systemic undermining of labor power since 1980. Shapiro proposes a progressive wealth tax (1% at $1-5M up to 5-7% above $1B) directed into sovereign wealth funds as the minimum viable intervention to redirect the trajectory toward technoabundance, arguing this creates a win-win-win where the rich get richer, government gets funded, and households get capital-based income.

## Key Points

- Technofeudalism is the default trajectory because neoliberalism has systematically undermined labor power for 45 years — the cyberpunk genre that predicted this (high-tech, low-life) was literally written as an extrapolation of 1980s corporatism, and metrics like the median age of first-time homebuyers exceeding 40 confirm we are already in the early stages. [09:37](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wngalQ32Cro&t=577)
- AlphaFold's release of 200 million proteins for free demonstrates how AI can completely obviate certain forms of intellectual property — work that would have cost trillions through traditional methods was done in essentially a weekend, suggesting that strong property rights may need fundamental rethinking in an age of artificial superintelligence. [27:17](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wngalQ32Cro&t=1637)
- A graduated wealth tax (1% for $1-5M households up to 5-7% above $1B) directed into sovereign wealth funds creates aligned incentives: the wealthy benefit because it puts money into consumers' pockets who then buy their products, government gets funded, and households build capital-based income — a win-win-win that avoids both revolutionary upheaval and the status quo drift toward technofeudalism.
