# Anthropic has crossed a line

## Executive Summary

David Shapiro analyzes Anthropic's upcoming Mythos model and the Glass Wing project — a responsible disclosure initiative giving early access to ~40 major tech companies so they can patch security vulnerabilities before public release. Mythos, rumored to be a 10 trillion parameter model, represents a step change in capability with benchmarks jumping 15-20%, and has already discovered thousands of zero-day exploits including a 27-year-old FreeBSD vulnerability and a 16-year-old FFmpeg exploit. Drawing on his background as an IT infrastructure engineer, Shapiro argues that while AI tools provide structural advantages to attackers in the short term, defenders have the home-field advantage long-term as the same tools are integrated into security vendor products, automated penetration testing, and email inspection — with humans remaining the weakest link in the chain.

## Key Points

- The Glass Wing project is Anthropic's unprecedented approach to responsible capability release: giving Mythos preview access to ~40 industry titans so they can patch vulnerabilities before general availability, after the model discovered thousands of zero-day exploits including bugs hidden for decades in critical infrastructure like FreeBSD and FFmpeg. [00:00:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ahRMR_9kqb0&t=0)
- AI coding tools provide structural short-term advantages to attackers because they can adapt more quickly, but over time the same tools get integrated into every security vendor's stack — firewalls, email inspectors, penetration testing — raising the baseline security standard through consistent, non-complacent automated monitoring. [07:58](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ahRMR_9kqb0&t=478)
- Humans remain the permanent weakest link in cybersecurity (the "layer 8 issue") — the most expensive breaches come from phishing and social engineering, and AI tools will eventually automate many of the safety behaviors that humans perform inconsistently, much like how driver-assist cameras automate safe driving habits. [10:02](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ahRMR_9kqb0&t=602)
