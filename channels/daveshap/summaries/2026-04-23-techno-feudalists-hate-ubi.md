# Techno-feudalists hate UBI

## Executive Summary

The video critiques the 'tech right' opposition to Universal Basic Income (UBI), arguing that their concerns about flattened incentives and lost productivity are unfounded. The speaker contends that historical innovators thrived due to financial security, suggesting UBI would actually maximize human potential and entropy generation rather than stifle it. Furthermore, the argument posits that UBI stimulates economic demand, which is necessary to drive energy consumption and ascend the Kardashev scale, countering the claim that it leads to total state dependence.

## Key Points

- Financial security provided by UBI enables high-level innovation by preventing the 'lost Einstein' phenomenon, as historical figures like Darwin and Gates required stability to pursue groundbreaking work [00:05:01](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ifq1G3tNp3c&t=301).
- UBI acts as a demand-side stimulus that forces the economy to produce more goods and services, thereby increasing the energy budget required to ascend the Kardashev scale [00:07:15](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ifq1G3tNp3c&t=435).
- Critics' fears of total state dependence are exaggerated, as UBI is designed as a baseline floor rather than a replacement for all other income sources or voluntary striving [00:09:09](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ifq1G3tNp3c&t=549).
