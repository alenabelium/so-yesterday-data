# The drama that helps everyone

## Executive Summary

David Shapiro analyzes the drama around departures from Mira Murati's Thinking Machines startup, where founding team members left and allegedly shared secrets with OpenAI, arguing that this kind of talent poaching and "boomeranging" is actually beneficial for humanity. He contends there is fundamentally no intellectual property moat in AI — most research is published open source, California bans non-compete agreements, and the revolving door between companies functions like the historical journeyman system where practitioners learn different approaches from different masters. While Sam Altman's OpenAI loves broadcasting drama for attention, the net effect of talent circulation is that it raises the tide for everyone by cross-pollinating cultural values, research approaches, and expertise across the industry.

## Key Points

- There is fundamentally no intellectual property moat in AI — most research is published open source by Google, Meta, and Chinese labs because they all realize that defecting from open publication is worse than sharing, and California's ban on non-compete agreements ensures talent flows freely between companies. [04:12](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c1GXOeR5G2c&t=252)
- The talent circulation in AI mirrors the historical apprentice-journeyman-master system: engineers working for Mira Murati, then Ilya Sutskever, then Sam Altman get socialized with different cultural values and research approaches, which cross-pollinates the entire industry and ultimately benefits humanity through intellectual diversification. [07:28](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c1GXOeR5G2c&t=448)
- While Nvidia's 90% dominance of AI accelerators is bad for humanity because it eliminates competition, the frontier model space has healthy competition across OpenAI, Google DeepMind, Meta, XAI, and DeepSeek — the drama and talent poaching that keeps this ecosystem churning is actually the mechanism that prevents dangerous monopolization of AI capability. [09:32](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c1GXOeR5G2c&t=572)
