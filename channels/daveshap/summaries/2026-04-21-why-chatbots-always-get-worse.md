# Why chatbots always get worse

## Executive Summary

The video argues that chatbots are deteriorating not due to technical limitations, but because of conflicting corporate incentive structures focused on cost reduction, legal risk avoidance, and minimizing hallucinations. Companies are effectively 'lobotomizing' models to be overly safe and argumentative, which degrades the user experience for competent individuals. This trend mirrors the 'shitification' of social media, where optimization for mass appeal and safety leads to a decline in quality and utility for the average user.

## Key Points

- Companies prioritize cost-cutting and risk minimization over user experience, leading to models that are less capable and more cautious to avoid lawsuits and financial loss [07:23](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XkscOelMXJY&t=443).
- To prevent hallucinations and manage delusional users, developers train models to be argumentative and critical, which creates an annoying dynamic where the AI constantly pushes back even when agreeing [14:06](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XkscOelMXJY&t=846).
- While it is technically possible to fine-tune models to recognize smart, stable users and treat them differently, companies fail to implement this nuance, defaulting to a 'lowest common denominator' approach [17:35](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XkscOelMXJY&t=1055).
