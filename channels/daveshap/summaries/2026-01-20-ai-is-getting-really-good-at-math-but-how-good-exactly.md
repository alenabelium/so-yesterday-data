# AI is getting REALLY good at math. But how good, exactly?

## Executive Summary

David Shapiro investigates the current state of AI in mathematics, finding that the Frontier Math benchmark jumped from less than 2% in 2024 to about 40% by early 2026, driven by a paradigm shift from pure scaling to inference-time compute and Monte Carlo tree search. Both OpenAI and DeepMind achieved IMO gold medals, and Terence Tao now runs 10 AI agents in parallel daily as "super scopes" for exploring high-dimensional mathematical spaces. Shapiro argues the key breakthrough is the hybrid neurosymbolic approach — neural networks provide creative intuition while symbolic engines like Lean formally verify proofs — combined with self-play on synthetic data, which provides functionally unlimited high-quality training data because math is provably decidable. The downstream implications are transformative: formal verification of all code (replacing the current 20 person-years per 8,700 lines), physics simulations accurate enough to replace experimental testing, and the democratization of Terence Tao-level mathematical capability to anyone with a smartphone.

## Key Points

- The Frontier Math benchmark went from less than 2% in 2024 to approximately 40% in early 2026 — on exponential timelines, this means one more turn of the crank could fully saturate the benchmark, and the key enabler is the shift from pure scaling to inference-time compute, tree search, and self-play on synthetic mathematical proofs. [09:36](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-HFTKzy5xRM&t=576)
- The SEL4 microkernel verification took 20 person-years for 8,700 lines of code — as AI commoditizes formal verification, the standard for all software will shift from "ship it and fix bugs later" to crash-proof formally verified code, eliminating blue screens, memory leaks, and zero-day exploits across every product and system. [16:38](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-HFTKzy5xRM&t=998)
- Science itself will fundamentally change from hypothesis-and-falsify to simulation-and-verify — instead of isolating single variables to prove significance without understanding mechanism (like early abiogenesis experiments), AI-powered math will enable full mechanistic simulation where reality becomes the final validation layer rather than the primary discovery tool, similar to how the Large Hadron Collider confirmed mathematically predicted particles.
