# The next 36 months will be WILD

## Executive Summary

David Shapiro surveys the converging predictions from Sam Altman, Jensen Huang, and Dario Amodei that AGI and recursive self-improvement will arrive in the 2027-2028 window. He breaks down the five components needed for recursive self-improvement — algorithmic research, data generation, code writing, model training, and model evaluation — arguing that the first three are largely solved while the last two remain bottlenecks. Shapiro highlights the "alligator jaws" of energy demand versus production and the emerging labor crisis where GDP growth decouples from job creation, calling 2025 the first year this was dramatically observable. He warns that the automation cliff is not mass firings but the vanishing of positions that were never created, with new graduate job latency already exceeding a year.

## Key Points

- The METR autonomous task benchmark shows a super-exponential trend, with Claude Opus 4.6 reaching 14.5 hours median autonomous success and the doubling period shortening to roughly 90 days — meaning we could see 60-120 hours of machine autonomy by year's end. [01:04](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zeHTTXAWDUA&t=64)
- The "vanishing ladder" is the real automation signal: it is not mass layoffs but ghost jobs that never get created, entry-level hiring freezes, and new graduates facing over a year of job latency — a secular regime of jobless growth that is already underway. [24:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zeHTTXAWDUA&t=1440)
- Energy and high-bandwidth memory are the current infrastructure bottlenecks, but market forces are already solving them through microgrids, collocated natural gas, and massive retooling investments — much as the chip shortage was resolved in 2023-2025. [13:12](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zeHTTXAWDUA&t=792)
