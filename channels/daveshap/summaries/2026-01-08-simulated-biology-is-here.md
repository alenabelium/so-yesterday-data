# Simulated Biology is Here

## Executive Summary

David Shapiro explores the implications of simulated biology — from AlphaFold's protein predictions to rumored "Alpha Cell" projects — arguing that humanity could achieve full control over its own biology within 5-10 years. Rather than debating whether we should "play God," he frames the real question as how weird could humans get, walking through scenarios from genetic rejuvenation to interstellar adaptation. He cautions that Malthusian competitive dynamics could pressure everyone to modify themselves even against their preferences, and identifies qualia fading and phenomenal drift as the most serious risks — the possibility of losing subjective experience through incremental changes that each seem rational in the moment. Shapiro advocates for slow, incremental genetic modifications over cybernetic or mind-uploading approaches, applying a minimax strategy to minimize the worst possible outcomes.

## Key Points

- The most dangerous dynamic of biological self-modification is not any single change but the Red Queen hypothesis applied to human evolution: competitive pressure will incentivize adaptation even for those who prefer to stay unmodified, potentially driving the species toward unrecognizable forms through individually rational but collectively transformative choices. [06:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0iM8d6jvYN0&t=406)
- Qualia fading and phenomenal drift — the gradual erosion of subjective experience through incremental biological or cybernetic modifications — represent what Shapiro considers the most existentially threatening risk, illustrated through the "Johnny Silverhand syndrome" from Cyberpunk 2077 and the branching problem of mind uploading. [03:14](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0iM8d6jvYN0&t=194)
- Interstellar travel will require biological self-modification as a practical necessity: cosmic radiation in interstellar space is worse than within our solar system, and adapting to different planetary environments (gravity, light, atmosphere) means genetic engineering is not optional but mandatory for becoming a multi-system species. [09:42](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0iM8d6jvYN0&t=582)
