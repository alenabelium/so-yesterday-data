# 85% Of People Will be Unemployable

## Executive Summary

David Shapiro presents a detailed quantitative model estimating that labor force participation will drop from the current 62% to roughly 10-15% when AI and robots become better, faster, cheaper, and safer than humans at all tasks. He breaks down residual employment into a statutory floor (jobs legally required to be human, 1.5-2.3%), an authenticity premium (artisans, entertainers, care workers, 6-10%), and a demand-side expansion scenario that optimistically reaches 23-40% but collapses back down when constrained by the physics of attention — the hard cap of 24 hours per day that no technology can expand. The key insight is that human demand for goods and services is infinite, but human demand for other humans is not, and the attention economy follows extreme power laws where the top 1% of creators capture 50% of attention, making this a lottery rather than a labor market.

## Key Points

- The 24-hour day is immune to Jevons' paradox — unlike every other resource where efficiency increases consumption, time cannot be manufactured, creating a hard ceiling on the attention economy that caps viable global creator jobs at 2-5 million, making content creation a lottery rather than a labor market. [13:11](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kC49JCXP-zU&t=791)
- Even in the most optimistic demand-expansion scenario with 2.5x more leisure time, the model collapses to 10-15% labor force participation because AI erases all skill barriers — when everyone has AI-powered access to top 1% capability in any domain, supply floods every market and earnings collapse toward zero through rent dissipation. [22:03](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kC49JCXP-zU&t=1323)
- The fundamental economic reframe is that human demand for goods and services is infinite, but there is no law of physics or economics requiring those demands to be satisfied by human labor — consumers do not care if a human hand never touches their phone, movie, or medication, as long as it is cheaper, faster, better, and safer.
