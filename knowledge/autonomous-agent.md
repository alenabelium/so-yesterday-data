An autonomous agent is an advanced AI system designed to perceive its environment, reason through complex scenarios, and execute actions to achieve specific goals with minimal human intervention. Unlike traditional chatbots that respond passively to prompts, autonomous agents operate in a loop of observation, planning, and action. They leverage Large Language Models (LLMs) as their 'brain' to interpret context, decompose high-level objectives into sub-tasks, and utilize external tools or APIs to interact with the digital world.

The core capability of an autonomous agent lies in its ability to maintain state and iterate. If an action fails, the agent can analyze the error, adjust its strategy, and retry, effectively solving problems that require multi-step reasoning and adaptability. This shift from passive generation to active execution marks a fundamental transition in AI, enabling systems to manage workflows, conduct research, or even write and deploy code independently.

## Key Aspects

- **Goal-Oriented Execution**: Agents are driven by high-level objectives rather than single-turn queries, breaking down complex problems into manageable steps.
- **Tool Integration**: They possess the ability to call external functions, browse the web, query databases, and manipulate software environments to gather information or perform tasks.
- **Self-Correction**: Through reflection loops, agents can evaluate their own outputs, identify errors, and refine their approach without human oversight.
- **Memory and Context**: Effective agents maintain a persistent memory of past actions and interactions, allowing them to learn from history and maintain coherence over long sessions.
