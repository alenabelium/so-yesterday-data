AI infrastructure encompasses the physical and cloud computing systems that power AI model training, inference, and deployment — data centers, GPUs, networking, power grids, and the cloud platforms built on top of them. As AI models scale and agent workloads multiply, infrastructure has become a strategic bottleneck and a massive investment category.

The infrastructure story is often overlooked by AI practitioners focused on models and tools, but it shapes what's possible. GPU availability determines who can train frontier models. Inference costs determine which applications are economically viable. Data center capacity determines how many agents can run simultaneously. Understanding AI infrastructure is essential for anyone making strategic AI decisions — from [token economics](/knowledge/token-economics) to build-vs-buy choices.

## Key Aspects

- **The compute crunch** — demand for AI compute is growing faster than supply, creating a 36-month infrastructure crisis
- **Investment scale** — $650+ billion in AI infrastructure spending, with data center construction surpassing office building construction
- **Strategic asset** — compute access is becoming a competitive moat for companies and a geopolitical lever for nations
- **Efficiency pressure** — high infrastructure costs drive demand for smaller, more efficient models and optimized inference
