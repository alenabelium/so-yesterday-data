Domain translation is the skill of converting deep expertise in a specific field into instructions, specifications, and workflows that AI systems can execute. As AI becomes more capable at execution, the bottleneck shifts to people who understand both the domain problem and how to express it in terms AI can act on.

Domain translators are increasingly valuable because AI tools are general-purpose but domain problems are specific. A doctor, lawyer, or engineer who can translate their expertise into AI-readable specifications creates more value than either a domain expert who ignores AI or an AI expert who lacks domain knowledge. This role is directly embodied by [The Domain Translator](/knowledge/the-displaced-expert) persona. The [Domain Translator](/personas/06-domain-translator) persona captures this perfectly — the person who walks into a panicked executive meeting with tested results, not slides. They know the AI vendor's demo works on clean data but their claims processing has 15 years of edge cases. They can explain why a chatbot hallucinating medical advice differs from hallucinating a recipe.

## Key Aspects

- **Bidirectional fluency** — understanding both the domain vocabulary and AI capabilities well enough to bridge them
- **Tacit knowledge extraction** — the hardest part is articulating expertise that domain experts "just know" but haven't formalized
- **Workflow mapping** — translating existing domain workflows into AI-augmented versions, preserving critical constraints
- **[Intent engineering](/knowledge/intent-engineering)** — a related skill focused on expressing high-level intent in ways AI systems can reliably interpret
- **The intent gap** — Klarna's AI agent handles 2.3M conversations monthly and saved $60M, yet optimized for resolution speed rather than customer relationships; domain translators catch these misalignments between AI optimization targets and actual business intent
