Open source AI is the movement to develop and distribute AI models, training code, datasets, and tooling under open licenses. It ranges from open-weight models (like Meta's Llama family) where the trained model is freely available, to fully open-source projects where training code, data, and methodology are all shared.

The open source vs. closed source debate in AI is one of the defining tensions of the field. Proponents argue that open models democratize access, enable innovation, and improve safety through transparency. Critics worry about misuse, safety risks, and the economics of competing with well-funded closed labs. The emergence of [OpenClaw](/knowledge/openclaw) — an open-source autonomous AI project — brought these tensions to a head in early 2026.

## Key Aspects

- **Open weights vs. open source** — most "open" models share weights but not training data or full methodology
- **Ecosystem effects** — open models create ecosystems of fine-tuned variants, tools, and applications
- **Safety tradeoff** — transparency enables safety research but also enables misuse
- **Competitive dynamics** — open models pressure closed labs on pricing and force rapid iteration
