Agentic AI represents a paradigm shift from passive, reactive language models to active systems capable of autonomous goal pursuit. Unlike traditional chatbots that wait for a single prompt and return a response, agentic systems can perceive their environment, formulate multi-step plans, decompose complex tasks, and execute actions using external tools. They operate in a loop of observation, reasoning, and action, allowing them to navigate dynamic environments and adapt to unexpected obstacles without constant human intervention.

The core capability of an agentic system lies in its ability to bridge the gap between high-level intent and low-level execution. By leveraging techniques like task decomposition and tool use, these systems can break down ambiguous objectives into manageable sub-tasks, execute them sequentially or in parallel, and verify outcomes before proceeding. This autonomy transforms AI from a mere content generator into a functional worker capable of managing workflows, coding applications, or conducting research independently.

## Key Aspects
- **Autonomous Reasoning**: The ability to self-correct and iterate on plans based on feedback from the environment or tool outputs.
- **Tool Integration**: Seamless interaction with external APIs, databases, and software to perform real-world actions beyond text generation.
- **Task Decomposition**: Breaking down high-level goals into a sequence of executable sub-tasks to manage complexity.
- **State Management**: Maintaining context and memory across multiple interaction steps to ensure long-term goal coherence.
