Agentic workflows represent a paradigm shift from passive AI assistants to autonomous systems capable of independent action. Unlike traditional chatbots that wait for explicit prompts, agentic systems interpret high-level goals, decompose them into sub-tasks, and autonomously select and execute the necessary tools to achieve the outcome. This capability relies heavily on the model's ability to reason through multi-step processes, manage state, and self-correct based on the results of its actions.

The core of an agentic system lies in its loop of planning, acting, and observing. The agent formulates a plan, executes a step using an available tool (such as a code interpreter, search engine, or API), observes the output, and then decides whether to proceed, adjust the plan, or terminate. This iterative process allows agents to handle open-ended, complex problems that exceed the capabilities of single-turn prompting, effectively bridging the gap between human intent and digital execution.

## Key Aspects

- **Autonomous Planning**: Agents break down vague objectives into concrete, executable steps without constant human intervention.
- **Tool Integration**: Seamless interaction with external APIs, code environments, and databases to perform real-world actions.
- **Self-Correction**: The ability to analyze failure states, debug errors, and retry strategies to ensure task completion.
- **State Management**: Maintaining context and memory across multiple interaction turns to ensure coherent long-term goal pursuit.
