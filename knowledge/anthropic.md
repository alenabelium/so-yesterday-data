Anthropic is a leading AI safety and research company founded in 2021 by former OpenAI researchers Dario Amodei and Daniela Amodei. The organization is best known for developing the Claude family of large language models, which are distinguished by their focus on safety, interpretability, and helpfulness. Unlike competitors that prioritize rapid scaling alone, Anthropic integrates safety research directly into the model development lifecycle, aiming to create systems that are robust against misuse and aligned with human values.

The company's technical approach is heavily influenced by the concept of Constitutional AI, a method where models are trained to critique and refine their own outputs based on a set of written principles rather than just human feedback. This methodology, combined with their proprietary 'Constitutional' training data, aims to reduce hallucinations and improve the model's ability to handle complex, nuanced reasoning tasks. Their models are also noted for exceptionally large context windows, allowing them to process entire books or codebases in a single pass.

## Key Aspects
- **Constitutional AI**: A training framework where models self-correct based on a predefined set of principles to ensure safety and alignment.
- **Claude Models**: A series of frontier LLMs (Claude 2, 3, 3.5) known for high reasoning capabilities and reduced hallucination rates.
- **Safety-First Philosophy**: A core organizational mandate to develop AI that is interpretable, controllable, and beneficial to humanity.
- **Large Context Windows**: Technical capability to process massive amounts of text (up to 200k+ tokens) for deep analysis and retrieval.
